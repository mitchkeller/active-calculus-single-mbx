We simplify the expression
\begin{equation*} \frac{f(x+h)-f(x)}{h} = \frac{3+6\sqrt{x+h}-(3+6\sqrt{x})}{h} \end{equation*}
by multiplying both numerator and denominator by the conjugate and simplifying.
\begin{equation*} \frac{3+6\sqrt{x+h}-(3+6\sqrt{x})}{h} = \frac{6\sqrt{x+h}-6\sqrt{x}}{h} =\frac{(6\sqrt{x+h}-6\sqrt{x})(6\sqrt{x+h}+6\sqrt{x})}{h(6\sqrt{x+h}+6\sqrt{x})} \end{equation*}
\begin{equation*} = \frac{36\mathopen{}\left(x+h\right)-36x}{h(6\sqrt{x+h}+6\sqrt{x})} = \frac{36h}{h(6\sqrt{x+h}+6\sqrt{x})} = \frac{36h}{6 h(\sqrt{x+h}+\sqrt{x})} \end{equation*}
Finally, we can divide to get
\begin{equation*} =\frac{6}{\sqrt{x+h}+\sqrt{x}} \end{equation*}
That means A=6, while B=C=1.
Now, we can take
\begin{equation*} \lim_{h\to 0} \frac{6}{\sqrt{x+h}+\sqrt{x}} = \frac{6}{2\sqrt{x}} . \end{equation*}
