Solution 5.5.5.5.1.

SOLUTION
Multiplying by the least common denominator gives
\begin{equation*} 3x^{2}-10x+4 = A(x^2+4)+(Bx+C)(x - 5) \end{equation*}
Rearranging terms on the right hand side, yields
\begin{equation*} 3x^{2}-10x+4 = (A+B)x^2+(-5B+C)x + 4 A - 5C \end{equation*}
Now we equate the coefficients:
\begin{equation*} \begin{array}{rl} A+B \amp = 3 \\ -5B +C \amp = -10 \\ 4 A - 5C \amp = 4 \end{array} \end{equation*}
Solving the system gives \(A=1, \; B = 2\) and \(C= 0\) so the partial fraction decomposition is
\begin{equation*} \frac{3x^{2}-10x+4}{(x - 5)(x^2+4)} = \frac{1}{x - 5} + \frac{2x}{x^2+4} \end{equation*}
The definite integral is then
\begin{equation*} \displaystyle \int \frac{3x^{2}-10x+4}{(x - 5)(x^2+4)} dx = \ln\!\left(\left|x-5\right|\right) + 1\ln\!\left(x^{2}+4\right) +C \end{equation*}
