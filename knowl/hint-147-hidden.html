The integral involves \(\cot u\) and \(\csc u\) where the exponent of \(\cot u\) is odd and positive. So we rewrite in order to have a copy of \(\csc(3 x) \cot(3 x)\) and use a trigonometric identity to turn the remaining even powers of \(\cot(3 x)\) into an expression of \(\csc^2(3 x)\text{.}\) Then use u-substitution with \(u= \csc(3 x)\text{.}\)
Thus the integral can be rewritten as
\begin{equation*} \int 12\cot^{3}\!\left(3x\right)\csc^{3}\!\left(3x\right) \, dx = 12 \int \cot^2(3 x) \csc^2(3 x) \csc(3 x) \cot(3 x) \, dx \end{equation*}
\begin{equation*} = 12 \int \bigg( \csc^2(3 x)-1 \bigg) \csc^2(3 x) \csc(3 x) \cot(3 x) \, dx \end{equation*}
Now let \(u= \csc(3 x)\text{.}\)
Letting \(u= \csc(3 x)\) means that \(du= -3 \csc(3 x)\cot(3 x) \, dx\text{,}\) and thus \(dx= \frac{du}{-3\csc(3 x)\cot(3 x)}\text{.}\)
So the integral turns into
\begin{equation*} = 12 \int \bigg( u^2-1 \bigg) u^2 \csc(3 x) \cot(3 x) \frac{du}{-3\csc(3 x)\cot(3 x)} \end{equation*}
