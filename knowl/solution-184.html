Solution 2.7.3.8.1.

\(\frac{dy}{dx}\big|_{(6,3)}\text{.}\) Implicit differentiation yields:
\begin{equation*} 2y\frac{dy}{dx}= \frac{3 x^{2} (xy + 6) - x^{3}(x\frac{dy}{dx}+y)}{(xy + 6)^2}. \end{equation*}
Given the complexity of the above equation, we first want to substitute 6 for \(x\) and 3 for \(y\) (the coordinates of the point where we are constructing our tangent line), then solve for \(\frac{dy}{dx}\text{.}\) Substitution yields:
\begin{equation*} 2(3) \frac{dy}{dx} = \frac{3 (6^{2}) ((6)(3) + 6) - 6^{3}(6\frac{dy}{dx} + 3)}{((6)(3) + 6)^2}, \end{equation*}
or,
\begin{equation*} 6 \frac{dy}{dx} = \frac{2592 - 216 (6 \frac{dy}{dx} + 3)}{576}. \end{equation*}
Solving for \(\frac{dy}{dx}\text{,}\) we have:
\begin{equation*} \frac{dy}{dx} \approx 0.4091. \end{equation*}
