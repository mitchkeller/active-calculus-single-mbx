To find the inflection points, we find \(f''(x)\) and determine where it is zero.
\begin{equation*} f''(x) = {10 (5 x^2 + 1) - 100 x^2\over(5 x^2+1)^2} = {10( 1 - 5 x^2 )\over(5 x^2+1)^2}. \end{equation*}
For this to be zero, we must have \(-1 + 5 x^2 = 0\text{,}\) or \(x = \pm 1/\sqrt{5}\text{.}\) There are exactly two inflection points, at \(x = -1/\sqrt{5} \approx -0.447\) and \(x = 1/\sqrt{5} \approx 0.447\text{.}\)
