To measure the average acceleration over an interval, we calculate the average rate of change of velocity over the interval. The units of acceleration are ft/s per second, or ft/s/s, written ft/s\({}^2\text{.}\)
\begin{equation*} \mbox{average acceleration for } 0\le t\le 1 = \frac{\mbox{Change in velocity}}{\mbox{Change in time}}, \end{equation*}
so, reading the velocities from the table, we have
\begin{equation*} \frac{v(1) - v(0)}{1} = 34.09 - 0.00 = 34.09 \mbox{ft/s}{}^2. \end{equation*}
Similarly,
\begin{equation*} \mbox{avg accel for } 1\le t\le 2 = \frac{v(2) - v(1)}{2-1} = 59.09 - 34.09 = 25 \mbox{ft/s}{}^2. \end{equation*}
