Draw a picture and introduce variables. It is essential to first understand what quantities are allowed to vary in the problem and then to represent those values with variables. Constructing a figure with the variables labeled is almost always an essential first step. Sometimes drawing several diagrams can be especially helpful to get a sense of the situation. A nice example of this can be seen in this applet 1 , where the choice of where to bend a piece of wire into the shape of a rectangle determines both the rectangle's shape and area.
