We find that
\begin{equation*} \cos(x^2) = 1 - \frac{1}{2!}x^4 + \frac{1}{4!}x^8 - \frac{1}{6!}x^{12} + \cdots\text{,} \end{equation*}
so
\begin{equation*} \int_0^1 \cos(x^2) \, dx = \left. x - \frac{1}{5 \cdot 2!}x^5 + \frac{1}{9 \cdot 4!}x^9 - \frac{1}{13 \cdot 6!}x^{13} + \cdots \right|_0^1\text{.} \end{equation*}
Thus,
\begin{equation*} \int_0^1 \cos(x^2) \, dx = 1 - \frac{1}{5 \cdot 2!} + \frac{1}{9 \cdot 4!} - \frac{1}{13 \cdot 6!} + \cdots \text{.} \end{equation*}
Since \(\frac{1}{13 \cdot 6!} = \frac{1}{9360} \approx 0.0001068\text{,}\) the next term is less than \(0.0001\) and thus we have
\begin{equation*} \int_0^1 \cos(x^2) \, dx \approx 1 - \frac{1}{5 \cdot 2!} + \frac{1}{9 \cdot 4!} - \frac{1}{13 \cdot 6!} = \frac{25399}{28080} = 0.904522792022792022792\ldots \text{.} \end{equation*}
A computational device reports that \(\int_0^1 \cos(x^2) \, dx \approx 0.914524\text{.}\)
