Definition 8.2.17.

Let \(n\) be a natural number and let \(f\) be a function with at least \(n\) derivatives at \(a\text{.}\) The degree \(n\) Taylor polynomial of \(f\) centered at \(a\) is the function
\begin{equation*} T_n(x) = c_0 + c_1(x-a) + c_2(x-a)^2 + \cdots + c_n(x-a)^n \end{equation*}
that satisfies \(T_n(a) = f(a)\text{,}\) \(T_n'(a) = f'(a)\text{,}\) \(T_n''(a) = f''(a)\text{,}\) \(\ldots\text{,}\) \(T_n^{(n)}(a) = f^{(n)}(a)\text{.}\)
