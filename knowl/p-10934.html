In (b), we just observed that the estimates of \(\ln(1.5)\) generated by \(T_5(x)\) and \(P_5(x)\) are the same. This occurs because \(f(x) = \ln(1+x)\) is just a shifted version of \(g(x) = \ln(x)\) (\(f(x) = g(1+x)\text{,}\) so \(f\) is \(g\) shifted 1 unit left), so by changing where their respective Taylor polynomials are centered, we are using the same structure to generate each approximation.
