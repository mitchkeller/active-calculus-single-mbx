Once we choose the angle \(\theta\text{,}\) the two right triangles in the trapezoid are determined, and each has a horizontal leg of length \(\cos(\theta)\) and a vertical leg of length \(\sin(\theta)\text{.}\) Thus, the sum of the areas of the two triangles is \(\sin(\theta) \cos(\theta)\text{,}\) and the area of the rectangle between them is \(2\sin(\theta)\text{.}\) Hence, the area of the trapezoidal cross-section is \(A(\theta) = \sin(\theta) \cos(\theta) + 2 \sin(\theta)\text{.}\) Because the length of the trough is constant, the troughâ€™s volume will be maximized by maximizing cross-sectional area. Note, too, that the domain for \(\theta\) is \(0 \le \theta \le \frac{\pi}{2}\text{.}\)
