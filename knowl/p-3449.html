To find the inflection points, we find \(f''(x)\) and determine where it is zero.

\begin{equation*} f''(x) = {4 (2 x^2 + 1) - 16 x^2\over(2 x^2+1)^2} = {4( 1 - 2 x^2 )\over(2 x^2+1)^2}. \end{equation*}

For this to be zero, we must have \(-1 + 2 x^2 = 0\text{,}\) or \(x = \pm 1/\sqrt{2}\text{.}\) There are exactly two inflection points, at \(x = -1/\sqrt{2} \approx -0.707\) and \(x = 1/\sqrt{2} \approx 0.707\text{.}\)
