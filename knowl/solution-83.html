Solution 1.8.4.2.1.

\(x=3\text{,}\) we have \(a=3\text{.}\) Since the point with \(x=3\) lies on both the line and the curve, we have
\begin{equation*} f(a) = f(3) = 4(3) - 4 = 8. \end{equation*}
The approximation is an underestimate because the line lies under the curve. Since the linear function approximates \(f(x)\text{,}\) we have
\begin{equation*} f(3.2) \approx 4(3.2) - 4 = 8.8. \end{equation*}
