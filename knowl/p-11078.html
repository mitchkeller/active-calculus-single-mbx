When we plot \(T_{10}(x)\) and \(f(x) = \ln(2) - \ln(3-x)\) on the interval from \(1 \lt x \lt 3\text{,}\) we see that \(T_{10}\) and \(f\) are nearly indistinguishable for a considerable subset of the interval. This suggests that \(T(x)\) is the Taylor serise of \(f(x) = \ln(2) - \ln(3-x)\text{.}\)
