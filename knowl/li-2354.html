Item c.
Since \(p'(x) = 3x^2 - a\) and \(a\) is constant, it follows that \(p''(x) = 6x\text{.}\) Note that \(p''(x) = 0\) when \(x = 0\) and that \(p''(x) \lt 0\) for \(x \lt 0\) and \(p''(x)\gt 0\) for \(x\gt 0\text{.}\) Hence \(p\) is CCD for \(x \lt 0\) and \(p\) is CCU for \(x\gt 0\text{,}\) making \(x = 0\) an inflection point.
