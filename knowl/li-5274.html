Item b.
In Section 8.4, we used Definition 8.4.1 to find the Taylor series expansion for \(h(x) = \ln(1+x)\text{.}\) Here we use substitution and integration to develop the Taylor series of \(h(x)\) in a different way.
  1. State the Taylor series expansion for \(p(u) = \frac{1}{1-u}\text{.}\)
  2. Let \(u = -x\) and use the series in (i) to find the Taylor series expansion for \(r(x) = \frac{1}{1+x}\text{.}\)
  3. Recall that
    \begin{equation*} \ln(1 + x) = \int_0^x \frac{1}{1+t} \, dt\text{.} \end{equation*}
    Use this result to find the Taylor series for \(h(x) = \ln(1+x)\text{.}\)
