The integral involves \(\cot u\) and \(\csc u\) where the exponent of \(\cot u\) is odd and positive. So we rewrite in order to have a copy of \(\csc(-2 x) \cot(-2 x)\) and use a trigonometric identity to turn the remaining even powers of \(\cot(-2 x)\) into an expression of \(\csc^2(-2 x)\text{.}\) Then use u-substitution with \(u= \csc(-2 x)\text{.}\)
Thus the integral can be rewritten as
\begin{equation*} \int 12\cot^{3}\mathopen{}\left(-2x\right)\csc^{3}\mathopen{}\left(-2x\right) \, dx = 12 \int \cot^2(-2 x) \csc^2(-2 x) \csc(-2 x) \cot(-2 x) \, dx \end{equation*}
\begin{equation*} = 12 \int \bigg( \csc^2(-2 x)-1 \bigg) \csc^2(-2 x) \csc(-2 x) \cot(-2 x) \, dx \end{equation*}
Now let \(u= \csc(-2 x)\text{.}\)
Letting \(u= \csc(-2 x)\) means that \(du= + 2 \csc(-2 x)\cot(-2 x) \, dx\text{,}\) and thus \(dx= \frac{du}{ + 2\csc(-2 x)\cot(-2 x)}\text{.}\)
So the integral turns into
\begin{equation*} = 12 \int \bigg( u^2-1 \bigg) u^2 \csc(-2 x) \cot(-2 x) \frac{du}{ + 2\csc(-2 x)\cot(-2 x)} \end{equation*}
