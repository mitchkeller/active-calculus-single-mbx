We first relate \(V\) and \(h\text{.}\) We know that \(V = \frac{1}{3} \pi r^2 h\text{,}\) as well as that for every time, \(t\text{,}\) the height and radius are equal so \(r = h\text{.}\) Thus,
\begin{equation*} V = \frac{1}{3} \pi h^2 h = \frac{1}{3} \pi h^3 \end{equation*}
Differentiating both sides of this equation with respect to \(t\)
\begin{equation*} \frac{dV}{dt} = \frac{1}{3} \pi \cdot 3h^2 \frac{dh}{dt} = \pi h^2 \frac{dh}{dt}\text{.} \end{equation*}
We note that at the instant \(V = 1000\text{,}\) \(1000 = \frac{1}{3} \pi h^3\text{,}\) so \(h^3 = \frac{3000}{\pi}\) and therefore \(h = \sqrt[3]{\frac{3000}{\pi}}\text{.}\) Recall also that \(\frac{dV}{dt} = 10\text{.}\) Applying all of this information at the instant \(V = 1000\) in the equation that relates \(\frac{dV}{dt}\) and \(\frac{dh}{dt}\text{,}\) we have
\begin{equation*} 10 = \pi \left( \sqrt[3]{\frac{3000}{\pi}} \right)^2 \left. \frac{dh}{dt}\right|_{V=1000}\text{.} \end{equation*}
Thus,
\begin{equation*} \left. \frac{dh}{dt}\right|_{V=1000} = \frac{10}{\pi \left( \sqrt[3]{\frac{3000}{\pi}} \right)^2} \approx 0.0328 \end{equation*}
feet per minute.
