The definition of the derivative is
\begin{equation*} f'(x)= \lim_{h\to 0} \frac{f(x+h)-f(x)}{h} \end{equation*}
This problem gives us the numerator, \(f(x+h)-f(x)\text{.}\) So,
\begin{equation*} f'(x)= \lim_{h\to 0} \frac{2 h x^2 - 2 h x - 8 h^2 x - 8 h^2 - 4 h^3}{h} = \lim_{h\to 0} 2 x^2 - 2 x - 8 h x - 8 h - 4 h^2 = 2 x^2 - 2 x \end{equation*}
