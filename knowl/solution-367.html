Solution 5.4.7.2.1.

Let \(u=x\) and \(dv=\sec^{2}\mathopen{}\left(-4x\right)dx\text{.}\)
Then \(du=dx\) and \(v=\frac{1}{-4} \tan(-4 x)\text{.}\)
The integration by parts formula says that
\begin{equation*} \int u \, dv = u\cdot v - \int v \, du \end{equation*}
So, substituting in, we get that
\begin{equation*} \int x \sec^{2}\mathopen{}\left(-4x\right) \, dx = x \cdot \frac{1}{-4} \tan(-4 x) - \int \frac{1}{-4} \tan(-4 x) \, dx \end{equation*}
\begin{equation*} =\frac{1}{-4} x \tan(-4 x) - \int \frac{1}{-4} \tan(-4 x) \, dx \end{equation*}
Unfortunately, the integral is not Basic, but we can rewrite it and integrate it using u-substitution.
\begin{equation*} \frac{1}{-4} x \tan(-4 x) - \int \frac{1}{-4} \frac{\sin(-4 x)}{\cos(-4 x)} \, dx \end{equation*}
Let \(u=\cos(-4 x)\) so \(du = + 4 \sin(-4 x) \, dx\) and solving for \(dx\) gives us \(dx=\frac{du}{ + 4 \sin(-4 x)}\text{.}\)
Substituting back into the integral gives us:
\begin{equation*} \int \frac{1}{-4} \frac{\sin(-4 x)}{\cos(-4 x)} \, dx = \int \frac{1}{-4} \frac{\sin(-4 x)}{u} \, \frac{du}{ + 4 \sin(-4 x)} \end{equation*}
\begin{equation*} = \int \frac{-1}{16} \frac{du}{u} \end{equation*}
This integrates to \(\frac{-1}{16} \ln(u)\) and substituting back in for \(u\) gives
\begin{equation*} \frac{-1}{16} \ln(\cos(-4 x))+C \end{equation*}
Combining it with the non-integrated term from integration by parts, we get the final answer:
\begin{equation*} \int x \sec^{2}\mathopen{}\left(-4x\right) \, dx = \frac{1}{-4} x \tan(-4 x) - \frac{-1}{16} \ln(\cos(-4 x))+C \end{equation*}
