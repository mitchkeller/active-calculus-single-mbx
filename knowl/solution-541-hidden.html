\(\displaystyle \frac{2}{1-4 x} = 2 \cdot \sum_{n=0}^\infty (4 x)^n\text{.}\) Then we can see that \(c_n = 2 \cdot 4^n\text{,}\) making \(c_0 = 2\text{,}\) \(c_1 = 2 \cdot 4 = 8\text{,}\) \(c_2 = 2 \cdot 4^2 = 32\text{,}\) etc. Regarding the radius of convergence, we perform the standard ratio test:
\begin{equation*} \lim_{n\to\infty} \left| \frac{2(4 x)^{n+1}}{2(4 x)^n} \right| = \lim_{n\to\infty} \left| 4 x \right| = \left| 4 x \right| \end{equation*}
And then \(\left| 4 x \right| \lt 1 \iff \left| x \right| \lt \frac 1 {4}\text{,}\) making \(R = \frac 1 {4}\text{.}\)
