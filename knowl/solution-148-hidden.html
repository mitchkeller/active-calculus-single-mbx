Using the chain rule, we know that \(h'(x) = f'(g(x)) \cdot g'(x)\text{.}\) We use slope to compute the derivatives. Since \(f(x)\) is linear on the interval \(0 \lt x \lt 2\text{,}\) we compute the slope of the line to see that \(f'(x) = 2\) on this interval. Similarly, we compute the slope on the interval \(2 \lt x \lt 4\) to see that \(f'(x) = -2\text{.}\) Since \(f(x)\) has a corner at \(x = 2\text{,}\) we know that \(f'(2)\) does not exist.
