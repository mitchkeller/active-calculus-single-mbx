\(\displaystyle \frac{4}{x}\) and its first three derivatives are \(f(x) = \displaystyle \frac{4}{x}\text{,}\) \(f'(x) = -{4\over x^2}\text{,}\) \(f''(x) = {8\over x^3}\text{,}\) and \(f'''(x) = -{24\over x^4}\text{.}\) Thus, evaluating these at \(x = -1\text{,}\) we get the terms term 0 = \({-4}\) term 1 = \(-{4}(x + 1)\) term 2 = \({-4}(x + 1)^2\text{,}\) and term 3 = \(-{4}(x + 1)^3\text{.}\) Thus the series is
\begin{equation*} \displaystyle \frac{4}{x} = {-4} - {4}(x + 1) + {-4}(x + 1)^2 - {4}(x + 1)^3 + \cdots. \end{equation*}
