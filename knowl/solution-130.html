Solution 2.3.5.6.1.

A. From the graphs, we find \(f(0) \approx -0.667\text{,}\) \(f'(0) \approx 0.667\text{,}\) \(g(0) = 0\text{,}\) and \(g'(0) \approx 1.333\text{.}\) By the product rule,
\begin{equation*} h'(0) = f'(0)\cdot g(0) + f(0)\cdot g'(0) \approx -0.889. \end{equation*}
B. Similarly, from the graphs, we find \(f(-2) = -1\text{,}\) \(f'(-2) = -4\text{,}\) \(g(-2) = -1\text{,}\) and \(g'(-2) = -2\text{.}\) By the quotient rule,
\begin{equation*} k'(-2) = \frac{f'(-2)\cdot g(-2)-f(-2)\cdot g'(-2)}{(g(-2))^2} = 2 \end{equation*}
