Solution 6.3.5.1.1.

(a) Suppose we choose an \(x\text{,}\) \(0\leq x\leq 3\text{.}\) If \(\Delta x\) is a small fraction of a meter, then the density of the rod is approximately \(\delta(x)\) anywhere from \(x\) to \(x+\Delta x\) meters from the left end of the rod. The mass of the rod from \(x\) to \(x+\Delta x\) meters is therefore approximately \(\delta(x) \Delta x = (5 + 3 x)\Delta x\text{.}\) If we slice the rod into \(N\) pieces, then a Riemann sum is \(\Sigma_{i=1}^{N}(5 + 3 x_i)\Delta x\text{,}\) or, more loosely, \(\Sigma (5 + 3 x)\Delta x\text{.}\) (b) The definite integral is
\begin{equation*} M=\int_0^{3} \delta(x)\,dx = \int_0^{3} (5 + 3 x)\,dx= (5 x + {3\over2} x^2)\bigg|_0^{3} = {57\over2} \hbox{ grams}. \end{equation*}
