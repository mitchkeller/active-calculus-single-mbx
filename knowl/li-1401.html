Item 2.2.3.3.c.
Using small positive and negative values of \(h\) to estimate the desired limits, we find that it appears
\begin{equation*} \lim_{h \to 0} \left( \frac{\cos(h)-1}{h} \right) = 0 \end{equation*}
and
\begin{equation*} \lim_{h \to 0} \left( \frac{\sin(h)}{h} \right) = 1 \end{equation*}
It suffices in each case to consider \(h\)-values of \(h = \pm 0.01, \pm 0.001, \pm 0.0001\text{.}\)
