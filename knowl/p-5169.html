The area of a circle is \(A=\pi r^2\text{.}\) So taking the derivative of both sides we get
\begin{equation*} \frac{dA}{dt}=2 \pi r \frac{dr}{dt} \end{equation*}
We are looking for \(\frac{dr}{dt}\text{,}\) and we are given that \(\frac{dA}{dt}=6\text{,}\) so we need to find \(r\text{.}\) But we know that \(A= 6\text{,}\) so
\begin{equation*} 6=\pi r^2 \end{equation*}
and thus
\begin{equation*} r=\sqrt{\frac{6}{\pi}}=1.38198 \end{equation*}
Substituting back in to \(\frac{dA}{dt}=2 \pi r \frac{dr}{dt}\) we get
\begin{equation*} 6=2\pi \cdot 1.38198 \frac{dr}{dt} \end{equation*}
and thus
\begin{equation*} \frac{dr}{dt}= \frac{6}{2\pi \cdot 1.38198 }=0.690988 \end{equation*}
