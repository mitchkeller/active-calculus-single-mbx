Answer 8.1.3.7.1.

  1. \(A_1 = \frac{1}{2}(100)\text{.}\)

  2. \(A_2 = \left( \frac{1}{2} \right)^2 \cdot 100\text{.}\)

  3. \(A_3 = \left( \frac{1}{2} \right)^3 \cdot 100\text{.}\)

  4. \(A_4 = \left( \frac{1}{2} \right)^4 \cdot 100\text{.}\)

  5. \(A_n = \left( \frac{1}{2} \right)^n \cdot 100 \text{.}\)

  6. \(A_n \to 0\) as time goes on.

  7. It takes about \(6.6439\) half-lives to elapse to get down to \(1\) gram remaining, or \(5 \cdot 6.6439 = 33.2193\) minutes.
