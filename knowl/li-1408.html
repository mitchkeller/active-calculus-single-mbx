Item 2.1.5.9.c.
Using the sum and scalar multiple rules, we have \(r'(x) = p'(x) + 2q'(x)\text{.}\) The slope of \(p\) at \(x=-2\) is \(-2\) and the slope of \(q\) at \(x=-2\) is \(3\text{,}\) so \(p'(-2) = -2\) and \(q'(-2) = 3\text{.}\) It follows that \('(-2) = p'(-2) + 2q'(-2) = (-2) + 2(3) = 4\text{.}\) Similarly, the slope of \(p\) at \(x=0\) is \(\frac{1}{2}\) and the slope of \(q\) at \(x=0\) is \(0\text{,}\) so \(p'(0) = \frac{1}{2}\) and \(q'(0) = 0\text{.}\) It follows that \(r'(0) = p'(0) + 2q'(0) = \frac{1}{2} + 2(0) = 1\text{.}\)
