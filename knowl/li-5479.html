Item.

An infinite geometric series \(S\) is a sum of the form
\begin{equation*} S = a + ar + ar^2 + \cdots + ar^{n-1} + \cdots\text{,} \end{equation*}
where \(a\) and \(r\) are real numbers. If \(|r| \lt 1\text{,}\) then the infinite geometric series converges to the finite value
\begin{equation*} S = \frac{a}{1-r}\text{.} \end{equation*}
If \(|r| \ge 1\text{,}\) then the infinite geometric series does not converge to a finite sum.
