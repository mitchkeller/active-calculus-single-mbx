Item 8.5.5.8.d.
Equating coefficients of like terms in Equation (8.5.5) and Equation 2, we find that \(3a_3 = a_2\text{,}\) \(4a_4 = a_3\text{,}\) and \(5a_5 = a_4\text{.}\) This implies that \(a_3 = \frac{1}{3}a_2 = \frac{1}{3 \cdot 2} = \frac{1}{3!}\text{,}\) \(a_4 = \frac{1}{4}a_3 = \frac{1}{4 \cdot 3!} = \frac{1}{4!}\text{,}\) and \(a_5 = \frac{1}{5}a_4 = \frac{1}{5 \cdot 4!} = \frac{1}{5!}\text{.}\) Thus we observe that \(a_k = \frac{1}{k!}\text{,}\) which is exactly what we’d expect for the Taylor series of \(e^x\text{.}\)
