SOLUTION
Multiplying by the least common denominator gives
\begin{equation*} x^{2}+5x-36 = A(x^2+9)+(Bx+C)(x+1) \end{equation*}
Rearranging terms on the right hand side, yields
\begin{equation*} x^{2}+5x-36 = (A+B)x^2+(B+C)x + 9 A+ C \end{equation*}
Now we equate the coefficients:
\begin{equation*} \begin{array}{rl} A+B \amp = 1 \\ B +C \amp = 5 \\ 9 A + C \amp = -36 \end{array} \end{equation*}
Solving the system gives \(A=-4, \; B = 5\) and \(C= 0\) so the partial fraction decomposition is
\begin{equation*} \frac{x^{2}+5x-36}{(x+1)(x^2+9)} = \frac{-4}{x+1} + \frac{5x}{x^2+9} \end{equation*}
The definite integral is then
\begin{equation*} \displaystyle \int \frac{x^{2}+5x-36}{(x+1)(x^2+9)} dx = -4\ln\!\left(\left|x+1\right|\right) + 2.5\ln\!\left(x^{2}+9\right) +C \end{equation*}
