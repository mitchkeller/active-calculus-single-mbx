SOLUTION

Multiplying by the least common denominator gives

\begin{equation*} 7x^{2}-8x+45 = A(x^2+9)+(Bx+C)(x - 4) \end{equation*}

Rearranging terms on the right hand side, yields

\begin{equation*} 7x^{2}-8x+45 = (A+B)x^2+(-4B+C)x + 9 A - 4C \end{equation*}

Now we equate the coefficients:

\begin{equation*} \begin{array}{rl} A+B \amp = 7 \\ -4B +C \amp = -8 \\ 9 A - 4C \amp = 45 \end{array} \end{equation*}

Solving the system gives \(A=5, \; B = 2\) and \(C= 0\) so the partial fraction decomposition is

\begin{equation*} \frac{7x^{2}-8x+45}{(x - 4)(x^2+9)} = \frac{5}{x - 4} + \frac{2x}{x^2+9} \end{equation*}

The definite integral is then

\begin{equation*} \displaystyle \int \frac{7x^{2}-8x+45}{(x - 4)(x^2+9)} dx = 5\ln\!\left(\left|x-4\right|\right) + 1\ln\!\left(x^{2}+9\right) +C \end{equation*}
