\begin{equation*} g(x) = g(2) + g'(2)(x-2) + \frac{g''(2)}{2!}(x-2)^2 +\frac{g'''(2)}{3!}(x-2)^3+\cdots \end{equation*}
Substituting gives
\begin{equation*} g(x) = 4 + 3(x-2) + \frac{-5}{2!}(x-2)^2 +\frac{5}{3!}(x-2)^3+\cdots \end{equation*}
(a) The degree 2 Taylor polynomial, \(P_2 (x)\text{,}\) is obtained by truncating after the \((x-2)^2\) term:
\begin{equation*} P_2(x) = 4+3\!\left(x-2\right)+\frac{1}{2!}\!\left(-5\right)\!\left(x-2\right)^{2}. \end{equation*}
(b) The degree 3 Taylor polynomial, \(P_3 (x)\text{,}\) is obtained by truncating after the \((x-2)^3\) term:
\begin{equation*} P_3 (x) = 4+3\!\left(x-2\right)+\frac{1}{2!}\!\left(-5\right)\!\left(x-2\right)^{2}+\frac{1}{3!}\cdot 5\!\left(x-2\right)^{3} \end{equation*}
(c) Substitute \(x=1.9\) into the Taylor polynomial of degree 2:
\begin{equation*} P_2(4.9) = 4+3\!\left(1.9-2\right)+\frac{1}{2!}\!\left(-5\right)\!\left(1.9-2\right)^{2} = 3.675. \end{equation*}
From the Taylor polynomial of degree 3, we obtain
\begin{equation*} P_3(4.9) = 4+3\!\left(1.9-2\right)+\frac{1}{2!}\!\left(-5\right)\!\left(1.9-2\right)^{2}+\frac{1}{3!}\cdot 5\!\left(1.9-2\right)^{3} = 3.67417. \end{equation*}
