Solution 1.5.4.1.1.

(a) As the cup of coffee cools, the temperature decreases, so \(f'(t)\) is negative. (b) Since \(f'(t) = dH/dt\text{,}\) the units are degrees Celsius per minute (degC/min). The quantity \(f'(35)\) represents the rate at which the coffee is cooling, in degrees per minute, 35 minutes after the cup is put on the counter. Thus we can complete the statement: At 35 minutes after the coffee was put on the counter, its temperature is 68 degC and will decrease by about 0.75 degC in the next 30 seconds.
