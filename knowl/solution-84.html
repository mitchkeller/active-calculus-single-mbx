Solution 1.8.4.3.1.

\(f(130) = 46\) and \(f'(130) = 1\) tell us that at \(x = 130\text{,}\) the value of the function is 46 and the function is increasing at a rate of 1 units for a unit increase in \(x\text{.}\) Since we increase \(x\) by -4.5 units in going from 130 to 125.5, the value of the function goes up by approximately \(-4.5 \cdot 1 = -4.5\) units, so
\begin{equation*} f(125.5) \approx 46 - 4.5 \cdot 1 = 46 - 4.5 = 41.5. \end{equation*}
