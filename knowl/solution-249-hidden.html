Solution: From the picture, we see that \(x\) is the width of the corral, and therefore the diameter of the semicircle, and that \(y\) is the height of the rectangular section. Thus the perimeter of the corral can be expressed by the equation \(2y+ x+ \frac{\pi}{2}x= 2y + (1+ \frac{\pi}{2})x= 524\) ft or equivalently, \(y=\frac{1}{2}(524 - (1+ \frac{\pi}{2})x)\text{.}\) Since \(x\) and \(y\) must both be nonnegative, it follows that \(x\) must be restricted to the interval \([0, \frac{524}{1+ \pi/2}]\text{.}\) The area of the corral is the sum of the area of the rectangle and semicircle, \(A= xy + \frac{\pi}{8}x^2\text{.}\) Making the substitution for \(y\) from the constraint equation, \(A(x)=\frac{1}{2}x(524 - (1+ \frac{\pi}{2})x) + \frac{\pi}{8}x^2 = 262 x - \frac{1}{2}(1+ \frac{\pi}{2})x^2 + \frac{\pi}{8}x^2\text{.}\) Now, \(A'(x) = 262 - (1 + \frac{\pi}{2})x + \frac{\pi}{4}x = 0\) implies \(x= \frac{262}{(1+ \frac{\pi}{4})} \approx 146.745978220028\text{.}\) With \(A(0)=0\text{,}\) \(A(\frac{262}{(1+ \frac{\pi}{4})}) \approx 19223.7231468237 \qquad\) and \(\qquad A(\frac{524}{1+ \frac{\pi}{2}}) \approx 16315.0015073775\text{,}\) it follows that the corral of maximum area has dimentions \(x= \frac{262}{1+ \frac{\pi}{4}} \qquad\) and \(\qquad y= \frac{1}{2}(524 - (1+ \frac{\pi}{2}) \frac{262}{1+ \frac{\pi}{4}}) \approx 73.372989110014\text{.}\)
