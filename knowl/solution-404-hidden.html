Over the interval \([0,\pi]\text{,}\) the graphs \(y = 2\sin x\) and \(y = 6 \cos x\) intersect when \(2\sin x = 6 \cos x\text{,}\) i.e., \(\tan x = \frac{6}{2}\text{,}\) i.e., \(x=\tan^{-1}(\frac{6}{2})=1.24904577239825\text{.}\) Let \(t = \tan^{-1}(\frac{6}{2})\text{.}\) Over the interval \(\left[0, t\right]\text{,}\) the graph of \(y = 2\sin x\) lies below that of \(y = 6 \cos x\text{.}\) Hence, the area between the two curves is \(\int_0^{ t}(6 \cos x-2 \sin x)dx\)
\begin{equation*} \begin{array}{ll} \amp =\left.(6\sin x+2\cos x)\right|_0^{ t} \cr \amp =\left(6\sin t+2\cos t\right)-(6\cdot 0+2\cdot 1)\cr \amp =\left(6\sin t+2\cos t\right)-2 \end{array} \end{equation*}
Since \(t = \tan^{-1}(\frac{6}{2})\text{,}\) we have \(\tan t = \frac{6}{2}\text{.}\) Considering a rightangle triangle with an angle which opposite side has length 6 and adjacent side has length 2, we see that the hypotenues is \(\sqrt{40}\text{.}\) Thus \(\sin t = \frac{6}{\sqrt{40}}\) and \(\cos t = \frac{2}{\sqrt{40}}\text{.}\) \(6\sin t+2\cos t = \frac{6^2}{\sqrt{40}} + \frac{2^2}{\sqrt{40}} = \frac{6^2+2^2}{\sqrt{40}} = \frac{40}{\sqrt{40}}=\sqrt{40}\text{.}\) Thus, \(\int_0^{ t}(6 \cos x-2 \sin x)dx= \sqrt{40}-2.\) Over the interval \(\left[ t,\pi\right]\text{,}\) the graph of \(y = 2\sin x\) lies above that of \(y = 6 \cos x\text{.}\) Hence, the area between the two curves is \(\int^\pi_{ t}(2\sin x - 6 \cos x)dx\)
\begin{equation*} \begin{array}{ll} \amp =-\left.(6\sin x+2\cos x)\right|^\pi_{ t} \cr \amp =2 + \sqrt{40} \end{array} \end{equation*}
Thus the total area is the sum of the above two integrals, which is \(2\sqrt{40}\text{.}\)
