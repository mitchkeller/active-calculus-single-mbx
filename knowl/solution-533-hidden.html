\begin{equation*} g(x) = g(2) + g'(2)(x-2) + \frac{g''(2)}{2!}(x-2)^2 +\frac{g'''(2)}{3!}(x-2)^3+\cdots \end{equation*}
Substituting gives
\begin{equation*} g(x) = 5 - 4(x-2) + \frac{1}{2!}(x-2)^2 +\frac{-4}{3!}(x-2)^3+\cdots \end{equation*}
(a) The degree 2 Taylor polynomial, \(T_2 (x)\text{,}\) is obtained by truncating after the \((x-2)^2\) term:
\begin{equation*} T_2(x) = 5+\left(-4\right)\mathopen{}\left(x-2\right)+\frac{1}{2!}\cdot 1\mathopen{}\left(x-2\right)^{2}. \end{equation*}
(b) The degree 3 Taylor polynomial, \(T_3 (x)\text{,}\) is obtained by truncating after the \((x-2)^3\) term:
\begin{equation*} T_3 (x) = 5+\left(-4\right)\mathopen{}\left(x-2\right)+\frac{1}{2!}\cdot 1\mathopen{}\left(x-2\right)^{2}+\frac{1}{3!}\mathopen{}\left(-4\right)\mathopen{}\left(x-2\right)^{3} \end{equation*}
(c) Substitute \(x=1.9\) into the Taylor polynomial of degree 2:
\begin{equation*} T_2(4.9) = 5+\left(-4\right)\mathopen{}\left(1.9-2\right)+\frac{1}{2!}\cdot 1\mathopen{}\left(1.9-2\right)^{2} = 5.405. \end{equation*}
From the Taylor polynomial of degree 3, we obtain
\begin{equation*} T_3(4.9) = 5+\left(-4\right)\mathopen{}\left(1.9-2\right)+\frac{1}{2!}\cdot 1\mathopen{}\left(1.9-2\right)^{2}+\frac{1}{3!}\mathopen{}\left(-4\right)\mathopen{}\left(1.9-2\right)^{3} = 5.40567. \end{equation*}
