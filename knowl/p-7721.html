  1. Set \(y\) equal to zero and look at how the derivative behaves along the \(x\)-axis.

  2. Do the same for the \(y\)-axis by setting \(x\) equal to \(0\)

  3. Consider the curve in the plane defined by setting \(y'=0\) -- this should correspond to the points in the picture where the slope is zero.

  4. Setting \(y'\) equal to a constant other than zero gives the curve of points where the slope is that constant. These are called isoclines, and can be used to construct the direction field picture by hand.
