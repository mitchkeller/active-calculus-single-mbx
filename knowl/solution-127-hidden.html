The function \(\displaystyle y=\frac{2x}{4x+7}\) is a quotient of two functions, say \(f(x)= 2x\) and \(g(x)=4x+7\text{.}\) Then taking the derivatives we get \(f'(x)=2\) and \(g'(x)=4\text{.}\) Finally, the quotient rule says that
\begin{equation*} \left[\frac{f(x)}{g(x)}\right]'=\frac{g(x)f'(x)-f(x)g'(x)}{\left[g(x)\right]^2} \end{equation*}
so
\begin{equation*} y'= \frac{(4x+7) \cdot (2) - (2x)\cdot (4)}{(4x+7)^2}= \frac{14}{(4 x + 7)^2}. \end{equation*}
