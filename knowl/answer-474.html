Answer 5.2.2.1.

  1. \(A'(x) = f(x)\text{.}\)
  2. \(A(1) = -\frac{\pi}{4}\text{.}\)
  3. \(A\) is increasing wherever \(f\) is positive; \(A\) is CCU wherever \(f\) is increasing. \(A(2) = 0\text{,}\) \(A(3) = -0.5\text{,}\) \(A(4) = -1.5\text{,}\) \(A(5) = -2\text{,}\) \(A(6) = -2 + \frac{\pi}{4}\text{,}\) and \(A(7) = -2 + \frac{\pi}{2}\text{.}\)
  4. \(F\) and \(A\) differ by the constant \(\frac{\pi}{4} - \frac{1}{2}\text{.}\)
  5. \(B\) and \(C\) have the same shape as \(A\) and \(F\text{,}\) and differ from \(A\) by a constant. Observe that \(B(3) = 0\) and \(C(1) = 0\text{.}\)
