By the sum rule for definite integrals over the same interval, we know that
\begin{equation*} \int_{-1}^{1} (3-x^2) \, dx - \int_{-1}^{1} 2x^2 \, dx = \int_{-1}^{1} [ (3-x^2) - 2x^2 ] \, dx \end{equation*}
and thus the latter single integral captures the exact area between the two curves on \([-1,1]\text{.}\)
