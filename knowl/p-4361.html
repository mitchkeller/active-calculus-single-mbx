The distance traveled is just the area under the curve. Note that in this case, this includes positive area (that above the \(t\)-axis), indicating forward motion, and negative area (that below the \(t\)-axis), indicating backward motion. The exact change in position is therefore

\begin{equation*} \Delta \mbox{position} = \frac12(3)(12) - \frac12(1)(4) = 16. \end{equation*}
