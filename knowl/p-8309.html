b. We slice the region into vertical rings. A typical ring has thickness \(\Delta x\text{,}\) outer radius \(y=1\) and inner radius \(y=x^{7}\text{.}\) Therefore,
\begin{equation*} \hbox{Volume of slice }\approx \pi 1^2\Delta x-\pi y^2\Delta x = \pi(1-x^{14})\, \Delta x. \end{equation*}
Therefore,
\begin{equation*} \hbox{Volume of solid } = \lim_{\Delta x\to0} \Sigma \pi(1-x^{14})\,\Delta x = \int_0^1\pi(1-x^{14})\, dx \end{equation*}
\begin{equation*} =\pi\left(x-\frac{x^{15}}{15}\right)\Bigg|_0^1 = \pi(1 - {1\over 15}) = {14\over15} \pi. \end{equation*}
