Solution 6.5.5.3.1.

\(1/v\) is undefined at \(v=0\text{.}\) To evaluate it, we must split the region of integration up into two pieces, from 0 to 3 and from \(-3\) to 0. But notice,
\begin{equation*} \int_0^{3}{1\over v}\, dv = \lim_{b\to 0^+}\int_b^{3} {1\over v}\, dv = \lim_{b\to 0^+}\left({\ln v}\bigg|_b^{3}\right) = \ln(3) - \ln b. \end{equation*}
As \(b\to 0^+\text{,}\) this goes to infinity and the integral diverges, so our original integral also diverges.
