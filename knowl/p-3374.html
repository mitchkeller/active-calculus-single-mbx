Note that \(r'(x) = g'(f(x)) \cdot f'(x) = \cos(x^3) \cdot 3x^2\text{.}\) Thus, we have \(r'(0.25) = \cos(0.25^3) \cdot 3(0.25)^2 \approx 0.1875\text{,}\) while using our work from (a), \(h'(0.25) = 3\sin^2(0.25) \cdot \cos(0.25) \approx 0.1779\text{,}\) so since \(|h'(0.25) \lt r'(0.25)|\text{,}\) we see that \(r\) is changing more rapidly.
