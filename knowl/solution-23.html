Solution 1.2.4.8.1.

  1. Using the standard formula for average velocity, we see that
    \begin{align*} AV_{[1,1+h]} &= \frac{s(1+h)-s(1)}{h}\\ &= \frac{(100\cos(0.75(1+h)) \cdot e^{-0.2(1+h)}+100) - (100\cos(0.75) \cdot e^{-0.2}+100)}{h}\\ &= \frac{100\cos(0.75(1+h)) \cdot e^{-0.2(1+h)}+100 - 100\cos(0.75) \cdot e^{-0.2}-100)}{h}\\ &= \frac{100\cos(0.75(1+h)) \cdot e^{-0.2(1+h)} - 100\cos(0.75) \cdot e^{-0.2}}{h} \end{align*}
  2. Using a spreadsheet and small values of \(h\) in the expression \(AV_{[1,1+h]}\text{,}\) we see that
    and hence
    \begin{equation*} \lim_{h \to 0} AV_{1, 1+h} \approx -53.837\text{.} \end{equation*}
  3. Since \(\lim_{h \to 0} AV_{1, 1+h} \approx -53.837\) this tells us that the instantaneous velocity of the bungee jumper at the moment \(t = 1\) is approximately \(-53.837\) ft/sec.
