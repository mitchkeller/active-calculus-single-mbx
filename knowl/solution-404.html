Solution 6.5.5.2.1.

\begin{equation*} \int_{2}^{\infty} 3x^{2}e^{-x^{3}} dx = \lim_{b \rightarrow \infty} \int_{2}^b 3x^{2}e^{-x^{3}} dx = \left.\lim_{b \rightarrow \infty} \left({{-3} \over 3} e^{-x^3}\right) \right|_0^b = \end{equation*}
\begin{equation*} \lim_{b \rightarrow \infty} \left({{-3} \over 3} e^{-b^3} - {{-3} \over 3} e^{-2^3}\right) = 0 + {3\over 3} e^{-8} = {3\over 3} e^{-8}. \end{equation*}
This integral converges to \({3\over 3} e^{-8}\text{.}\)
