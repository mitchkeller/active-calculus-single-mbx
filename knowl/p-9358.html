In this case we know that \(k^3 \lt k^3+1\) and so if \(k \gt 0\) then \(\frac{1}{k^3} \gt \frac{1}{k^3+1}\text{.}\) It follows that \(\frac{k}{k^3} \gt \frac{k}{k^3+1}\) and so the series \(\sum \frac{k}{k^3+1}\) is bounded below by 0 and above by the series \(\sum \frac{k}{k^3}\text{.}\) Now

\begin{equation*} \frac{k}{k^3} = \frac{1}{k^2} \end{equation*}

and so the series \(\sum \frac{k}{k^3}\) is a \(p\)-series with \(p=2\) and converges. The Direct Comparison Test shows that the smaller series \(\sum \frac{k}{k^3+1}\) must also converge.
