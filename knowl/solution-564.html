Solution 9.5.5.1.

\(x\) and \(y\) as being parametrically equations with parameter \(\theta\text{:}\)
\begin{equation*} x = r\cos(\theta) = 4 \sin(5\theta) \cos(\theta), \ \hbox{and}\ y = r\sin(\theta) = 4 \sin(5\theta) \sin(\theta). \end{equation*}
Then we know that the slope of the tangent line is \({dy\over dx} = {dy/d\theta\over dx/d\theta}\text{.}\) Taking the derivatives, \({dx\over d\theta} = 4(5\cos(5\theta)\cos(\theta) - \sin(5\theta)\sin(\theta))\) and \({dy\over d\theta} = 4(5\cos(5\theta)\sin(\theta) + \sin(5\theta)\cos(\theta))\text{.}\) Thus
\begin{equation*} {dy\over dx} = {5\cos(5\theta)\sin(\theta) + \sin(5\theta)\cos(\theta) \over 5\cos(5\theta)\cos(\theta) - \sin(5\theta)\sin(\theta) }. \end{equation*}
We want this at \(\theta = \pi/4\text{,}\) so that we get \({dy\over dx}\approx 1.5\text{.}\) Then, at \(\theta = \pi/4\text{,}\) \(x(\pi/4) = 4 \sin(5\cdot \pi/4)\cos(\pi/4) \approx -2\text{,}\) and \(y(\pi/4) = 4 \sin(5\cdot \pi/4)\sin(\pi/4) \approx -2\text{,}\) so that the tangent line is
\begin{equation*} y \approx -2 + (1.5)(x + 2). \end{equation*}
(Note, however, that the correct answer may require more significant figures than are shown in this solution.)
