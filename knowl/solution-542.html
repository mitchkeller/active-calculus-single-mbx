Solution 8.3.3.1.

  1. For \(1 + \frac{1}{3} + \frac{1}{9} + \frac{1}{27} + \cdots\text{,}\) we observe that \(a = 1\) and \(r = \frac{1}{3}\text{.}\) Thus,
    \begin{equation*} S_5 = \frac{a - ar^5}{1-r} = \frac{1 - (1/3)^5}{1-(1/3)} = \frac{121}{81} \approx 1.4938 \end{equation*}
    and
    \begin{equation*} S_{10} = \frac{a - ar^{10}}{1-r} = \frac{1 - (1/3)^{10}}{1-(1/3)} = \frac{29524}{19683} \approx 1.49997\text{.} \end{equation*}
    We see evidence in the partial sums that the infinite geometric series converges. Indeed, \(|r| = \frac{1}{3} \lt 1\text{,}\) so the series converges and its sum is
    \begin{equation*} S = \frac{a}{1-r} = \frac{1}{1-\frac{1}{3}} = \frac{1}{\frac{2}{3}} = \frac{3}{2}\text{.} \end{equation*}
  2. The sum \(4 - 2 + 1 - \frac{1}{2} + \frac{1}{4} - \cdots\) is geometric with \(a = 4\) and \(r = -\frac{1}{2}\text{.}\) Therefore,
    \begin{equation*} S_5 = \frac{a - ar^5}{1-r} = \frac{4 - 4\cdot(-1/2)^5}{1-(-1/2)} = \frac{11}{4} \end{equation*}
    and
    \begin{equation*} S_{10} = \frac{a - ar^{10}}{1-r} = \frac{4 - 4\cdot(-1/2)^{10}}{1-(-1/2)} = \frac{341}{128} = 2.6640625\text{.} \end{equation*}
    With \(|r| = \frac{1}{2} \lt 1\text{,}\) the infinite geometric series converges and its sum is
    \begin{equation*} S = \frac{a}{1-r} = \frac{4}{1+\frac{1}{2}} = \frac{4}{\frac{3}{2}} = \frac{8}{3}\text{.} \end{equation*}
  3. In the sum \(2 + \frac{8}{3} + \frac{32}{9} + \frac{128}{27} + \cdots \text{,}\) we notice that to get from one term to the next, say from \(\frac{8}{3}\) to \(\frac{32}{9}\text{,}\) we have to multiply by \(r = \frac{4}{3}\text{.}\) Thus, the geometric sum has \(a = 2\) and \(r = \frac{4}{3}\text{.}\) Since \(|r| = \frac{4}{3} \ge 1\text{,}\) the infinite series diverges, but we can still find the partial sums \(S_5\) and \(S_{10}\text{:}\)
    \begin{equation*} S_5 = \frac{a - ar^5}{1-r} = \frac{2 - 2\cdot(4/3)^5}{1-(4/3)} = \frac{1562}{81} \approx 19.284 \end{equation*}
    and
    \begin{equation*} S_{10} = \frac{a - ar^{10}}{1-r} = \frac{2 - 2\cdot(4/3)^{10}}{1-(4/3)} = \frac{1979054}{19683} \approx 100.546\text{.} \end{equation*}
  4. For the sum written in sigma notation, \(\sum_{k = 0}^{\infty} 5 \cdot \left( \frac{3}{4} \right)^k\text{,}\) itâ€™s helpful to write out the first several terms:
    \begin{equation*} \sum_{k = 0}^{\infty} 5 \cdot \left( \frac{3}{4} \right)^k = 5 + 5 \cdot \frac{3}{4} + 5 \cdot \frac{9}{16} + \cdots\text{.} \end{equation*}
    This shows that \(a = 5\) and \(r = \frac{3}{4}\) so
    \begin{equation*} S_5 = \frac{a - ar^5}{1-r} = \frac{5 - 5\cdot(3/4)^5}{1-(3/4)} = \frac{3905}{256} \approx 15.254\text{,} \end{equation*}
    \begin{equation*} S_{10} = \frac{a - ar^{10}}{1-r} = \frac{5 - 5\cdot(3/4)^{10}}{1-(3/4)} = \frac{4947635}{262144} \approx 18.874\text{,} \end{equation*}
    and with \(|r| = \frac{3}{4} \lt 1\) the sum of the convergent series is
    \begin{equation*} S = \frac{a}{1-r} = \frac{5}{1-\frac{3}{4}} = 20\text{.} \end{equation*}
  5. Expanding the infinite sum,
    \begin{equation*} \sum_{k = 1}^{\infty} -2 \cdot \left( -\frac{2}{3} \right)^k = \frac{4}{3} - \frac{8}{9} + \frac{16}{27} - \cdots \end{equation*}
    and we see that \(a = \frac{4}{3}\) and \(r = -\frac{2}{3}\text{.}\) Thus,
    \begin{equation*} S_5 = \frac{a - ar^5}{1-r} = \frac{\frac{4}{3} - \frac{4}{3} \cdot (-2/3)^5}{1-(-2/3)} = \frac{220}{243} \approx 0.905\text{,} \end{equation*}
    \begin{equation*} S_{10} = \frac{a - ar^{10}}{1-r} = \frac{\frac{4}{3} - \frac{4}{3} \cdot (-2/3)^{10}}{1-(-2/3)} = \frac{46420}{59049} \approx 0.786\text{,} \end{equation*}
    and with \(|r| = \frac{2}{3} \lt 1\) the sum of the convergent series is
    \begin{equation*} S = \frac{a}{1-r} = \frac{\frac{4}{3}}{1+\frac{2}{3}} = \frac{4}{5} = 0.8\text{.} \end{equation*}
