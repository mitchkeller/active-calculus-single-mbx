Solution 8.4.4.4.1.

\(3 \cos(x)\) and its first three derivatives are \(f(x) = 3 \cos(x)\text{,}\) \(f'(x) = -3 \sin(x)\text{,}\) \(f''(x) = -3 \cos(x)\text{,}\) and \(f'''(x) = 3 \sin(x)\text{.}\) Thus, evaluating these at \(x = -\pi/4\text{,}\) we get the terms term 0 = \({3\over\sqrt2}\) term 1 = \({3\over\sqrt2}(x + {\pi\over 4})\) term 2 = \(-{3\over2\sqrt2}(x + {\pi\over4})^2\text{,}\) and term 3 = \(-{{1\over2\sqrt2}}(x + {\pi\over4})^3\text{.}\) Thus the series is
\begin{equation*} 3 \cos(x) = {3\over\sqrt2} + {3\over\sqrt2}(x + {\pi\over 4}) - {3\over2\sqrt2}(x + {\pi\over4})^2 - {{1\over2\sqrt2}}(x + {\pi\over4})^3 + \cdots. \end{equation*}
