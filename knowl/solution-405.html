Solution 6.1.5.5.1.

Looking at the region, x starts at \(a=0\) and ends at \(b=1\text{,}\) and the higher y-value is the line \(y=1\text{,}\) while the lower y-value is \(y=\sqrt[4]{x}\text{.}\) So the integral is \(\displaystyle \int_0^1 \left[1-\sqrt[4]{x}\right] \ dx\)
Looking at the region again, y starts at \(c=0\) and ends at \(d=1\text{,}\) and x goes from 0 on the left side of the region to the graph of \(y=\sqrt[4]{x}\) on the right. But that has x in it, so we solve for x and get \(x=y^4\text{.}\) So the integral is \(\displaystyle \int_0^1 y^4 \ dy\)
