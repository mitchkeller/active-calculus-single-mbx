Equation (8.3.12) holds because when \(|r| \lt 1\text{,}\) it follows that \(r^n \to 0\) as \(n \to \infty\text{.}\) Indeed, when \(|r| \lt 1\text{,}\) we see by taking the limit as \(n \to \infty\) in Equation (8.3.7), that
\begin{equation*} \lim_{n \to \infty} S_n = \lim_{n \to \infty} \frac{a(1-r^n)}{1-r} = \frac{a}{1-r}\text{.} \end{equation*}
In Exercise 8.3.5.7, we will explore why the infinite geometric series does not converge when \(|r| = 1\) and when \(|r| \gt 1\text{.}\)
