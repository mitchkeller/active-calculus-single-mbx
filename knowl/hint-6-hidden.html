1. Remember that average velocity on an interval computes the quotient of “change in \(s\) over change in \(t\text{.}\)” This is the slope of the line between the corresponding two points on the graph of \(s\text{.}\)

  2. Think about shorter and shorter time intervals and drawing the lines whose slopes represent average velocity.

  3. Think about zooming in on the graph at \(t = 2\) and drawing a line that, up close, looks just like the curve \(s(t)\text{.}\) What is the approximate slope of that line?
