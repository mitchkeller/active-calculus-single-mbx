\(r=8 \sin 2\theta\) in cartesian coordinates:
With \(r_0=8\) We see that as \(\theta\) varies from \(0\) to \(\frac{\pi }{4}\) the radius \(r\) is increasing from \(0\) to \(8\text{,}\) and when \(\theta\) varies from \(\frac{\pi }{4}\) to \(\frac{\pi}{2}\text{,}\) \(r\) is decreasing back to zero. Hence, the leaf in the first quadrant is traced as \(\theta\) varies from \(0\) to \(\frac{\pi }{2}\text{.}\) The area of the leaf (the four leaves have equal areas) is thus
\begin{equation*} A=\frac{1}{2}\int_{0}^{\pi/2}{r}^{2} \,d\theta= \frac{1}{2}\int_{0}^{\pi/2} 64\sin^{2} 2\theta \,d\theta. \end{equation*}
Using the identity
\begin{equation*} \sin^{2} 2\theta =\frac{1- \cos 4\theta }{2} \end{equation*}
we get
\begin{equation*} A = 32\int_{0}^{\pi/2}\left(\frac{1}{2}-\frac{ \cos 4\theta }{2}\right) \,d\theta= 32\left(\frac{\theta }{2}-\frac{ \sin 4\theta}{8}\right)\bigg|_{0}^{\pi/2}= 32\left(\left(\frac{\pi}{4}-\frac{ \sin 2\pi }{8}\right)-0\right)= \frac{32\pi }{4} \end{equation*}
The area of one leaf is \(A=\frac{32\pi }{4}\approx 25.133\text{.}\)
