Solution 6.5.5.1.1.

\begin{equation*} \int_{0}^{\infty} 4xe^{-x^{2}} dx = \lim_{b \rightarrow \infty} \int_{0}^b 4xe^{-x^{2}} dx = \left.\lim_{b \rightarrow \infty} \left({{-4} \over 2} e^{-x^2}\right) \right|_0^b = \end{equation*}
\begin{equation*} \lim_{b \rightarrow \infty} \left({{-4} \over 2} e^{-b^2} - {{-4} \over 2} e^{-0^2}\right) = 0 + {4\over 2} e^{-0} = {4\over 2} . \end{equation*}
This integral converges to \({4\over 2}\text{.}\)
