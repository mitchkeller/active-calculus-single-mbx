Hint 5.3.5.1.1.

Remember that the derivative of \(\sec x\) is \(\sec x \tan x\text{.}\)
Let \(u = \sec\!\left(2x\right)\text{.}\) Don't forget the chain rule when you find \(du\text{.}\)
Remember that to integrate something of the form \(\displaystyle \frac{1}{u^2}\) you should rewrite as \(u^{-2}\) and then use power rule.
