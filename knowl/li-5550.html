Item b.
With \(f^{(k)}(0) = 1\) for every natural number \(k\text{,}\) in the the Taylor series, \(T_f(x)\) centered at \(a = 0\) of \(f(x) = e^x\text{,}\) we have \(c_k = \frac{f^{(k)}(0)}{k!} = \frac{1}{k!}\text{.}\) Thus
\begin{equation*} T_f(x) = \sum_{n=0}^{\infty} \frac{1}{k!} x^k = 1 + x + \frac{1}{2!}x^2 + \frac{1}{3!}x^3 + \cdots + \frac{1}{n!}x^n + \cdots \end{equation*}
