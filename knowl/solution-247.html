Solution 3.4.3.4.1.

A rectangle has sides \(x\) and \(y\text{.}\) Since the names of the variables don’t matter, say one of the \(y\) sides costs 12 dollars per foot and the other one and the two sides of length \(x\) all cost 4 dollars per square foot. Then the total cost of the fencing is
\begin{equation*} C=4 x + 4 x + 4 y + 12 y= 8 x + 16 y \end{equation*}
We are trying to minimize this function, but we need to reduce it to one variable. However, we are also given that the area of this rectangle is 230. Thus, \(xy=230\text{,}\) and solving for one of the variables, \(y=\frac{230}{x}\text{.}\)
Substituting back into the cost function, we get
\begin{equation*} C= 8 x+ 16 \left(\frac{230}{x} \right) = 8 x+ 3680 x^{-1} \end{equation*}
Take the derivative and set it equal to 0.
\begin{equation*} C'=8 - 3680 x^{-2}= 8 + \frac{-3680}{x^2}=0 \end{equation*}
Multiply both sides by \(x^2\)
\begin{equation*} 8 x^2-3680=0 \end{equation*}
and solve
\begin{equation*} x=\sqrt{\frac{3680}{8}}=21.4476105895272 \mbox{ ft.} \end{equation*}
(Why didn’t I know that it wasn’t \(x=-\sqrt{\frac{3680}{8}}=-21.4476105895272\) ? )
Finally, \(y=\frac{230}{x}\) so \(y=\frac{230}{21.4476105895272}=10.7238052947636 \mbox{ ft}\text{.}\)
