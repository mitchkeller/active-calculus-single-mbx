You can do the next step in different ways, but one way is to solve for \(dx\text{.}\) Since \(du = 2\csc^{2}\mathopen{}\left(-2x\right) \, dx\text{,}\) we divide over to get that
\begin{equation*} dx=\frac{1}{2\csc^{2}\mathopen{}\left(-2x\right)} \, du \end{equation*}
Then we substitute back into the original integral.
\begin{equation*} \int 6 \csc^2(-2 x) e^{\cot(-2 x)} \, dx = \int 6 \csc^2(-2 x) e^{u} \frac{1}{2\csc^{2}\mathopen{}\left(-2x\right)} \, du \end{equation*}
Which simplifies to
\begin{equation*} \int 3e^{u}du \end{equation*}
