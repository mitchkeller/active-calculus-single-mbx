To find the equilibrium solutions, we set \(\frac{dv}{dt} = 0\) and solve for \(v\text{.}\) Doing so, \(0 = 9.8 - kv\text{,}\) so \(v = \frac{9.8}{k}\text{.}\) Moreover, we note that for \(v \lt \frac{9.8}{k}\text{,}\) \(\frac{dv}{dt} \gt 0\text{,}\) while for \(\frac{dv}{dt} \gt \frac{9.8}{k}\text{,}\) \(\frac{dv}{dt} \lt 0\text{.}\) Thus, for velocities just above the equilibrium, the velocity will decrease and for velocities just below the equilibrium, the velocity will decrease, making \(v = \frac{9.8}{k}\) a stable equilibrium.
