Item b.
Observe that by the product rule, \(m'(v) = \sin(v^2) \frac{d}{dv}[\cos(v^3)] + \cos(v^3) \frac{d}{dv}[\sin(v^2)]\text{.}\) Applying the chain rule to differentiate \(\cos(v^3)\) and \(\sin(v^2)\text{,}\) we see that
\begin{equation*} m'(v) = \sin(v^2) [-\sin(v^3) \cdot 3v^2] + \cos(v^3) [\cos(v^2) \cdot 2v] = -3v^2 \sin(v^2)\sin(v^3) + 2v \cos(v^3)\cos(v^2)\text{.} \end{equation*}
