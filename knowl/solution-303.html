Solution 4.3.5.6.1.

\(\int_0^2\,f(x)\,dx\) and \(\int_0^2\,g(x)\,dx\) as the area under the curves and dividing by the length of the interval, which is 2. This gives us: \(\hbox{Average}(f) = 0.25\text{,}\) and \(\hbox{Average}(g) = 0.25\text{.}\) However, when we find \(f(x)\cdot g(x)\text{,}\) we find that the product is everywhere zero! \(g(x)\) is zero for \(0\le x\le 1\text{,}\) so \(f(x)\cdot g(x)\) is zero there, and \(g(x)\) is zero for \(1\le x\le 2\text{,}\) so \(f(x)\cdot g(x)\) is zero there too. Thus the average value of the product is zero, and the average of the product is not equal to the product of the averages!
