\begin{equation*} g(x) = g(5) + g'(5)(x-5) + \frac{g''(5)}{2!}(x-5)^2 +\frac{g'''(5)}{3!}(x-5)^3+\cdots \end{equation*}
Substituting gives
\begin{equation*} g(x) = 2 + 4(x-5) + \frac{2}{2!}(x-5)^2 +\frac{3}{3!}(x-5)^3+\cdots \end{equation*}
(a) The degree 2 Taylor polynomial, \(P_2 (x)\text{,}\) is obtained by truncating after the \((x-5)^2\) term:
\begin{equation*} P_2(x) = 2+4\!\left(x-5\right)+\frac{1}{2!}\cdot 2\!\left(x-5\right)^{2}. \end{equation*}
(b) The degree 3 Taylor polynomial, \(P_3 (x)\text{,}\) is obtained by truncating after the \((x-5)^3\) term:
\begin{equation*} P_3 (x) = 2+4\!\left(x-5\right)+\frac{1}{2!}\cdot 2\!\left(x-5\right)^{2}+\frac{1}{3!}\cdot 3\!\left(x-5\right)^{3} \end{equation*}
(c) Substitute \(x=4.9\) into the Taylor polynomial of degree 2:
\begin{equation*} P_2(4.9) = 2+4\!\left(4.9-5\right)+\frac{1}{2!}\cdot 2\!\left(4.9-5\right)^{2} = 1.61. \end{equation*}
From the Taylor polynomial of degree 3, we obtain
\begin{equation*} P_3(4.9) = 2+4\!\left(4.9-5\right)+\frac{1}{2!}\cdot 2\!\left(4.9-5\right)^{2}+\frac{1}{3!}\cdot 3\!\left(4.9-5\right)^{3} = 1.6095. \end{equation*}
