Solution 8.3.2.1.

  1. We see that
    \begin{equation*} \frac{2}{5} \cdot S_n = \frac{2}{5} \left( 1 + \frac{2}{5} + \left(\frac{2}{5}\right)^2 + \cdots + \left(\frac{2}{5}\right)^{n-1} \right) \text{,} \end{equation*}
    or
    \begin{equation} \frac{2}{5} \cdot S_n = \frac{2}{5} + \left(\frac{2}{5}\right)^2 \left(\frac{2}{5}\right)^3 + \cdots + \left(\frac{2}{5}\right)^n \text{.}\tag{8.3.5} \end{equation}
  2. Next, we use EquationÂ (8.3.5) in the expression \(S_n - \frac{2}{5} \cdot S_n\) to find that
    \begin{align*} S_n - \frac{2}{5} \cdot S_n =\mathstrut \amp \left( 1 + \frac{2}{5} + \left(\frac{2}{5}\right)^2 + \cdots + \left(\frac{2}{5}\right)^{n-1} \right) - \left(\frac{2}{5} + \left(\frac{2}{5}\right)^2 + \left(\frac{2}{5}\right)^3 + \cdots + \left(\frac{2}{5}\right)^n \right)\\ =\mathstrut \amp 1 + \frac{2}{5} + \left(\frac{2}{5}\right)^2 + \cdots + \left(\frac{2}{5}\right)^{n-1} - \frac{2}{5} - \left(\frac{2}{5}\right)^2 - \left(\frac{2}{5}\right)^3 - \cdots - \left(\frac{2}{5}\right)^n \right)\\ =\mathstrut \amp 1 - \left(\frac{2}{5}\right)^n \end{align*}
  3. Using the fact that \(S_n - \frac{2}{5} \cdot S_n = \frac{3}{5} \cdot S_n\text{,}\) we see that
    \begin{equation*} \frac{3}{5} \cdot S_n = 1 - \left(\frac{2}{5}\right)^n \end{equation*}
    so
    \begin{equation*} S_n = \frac{1 - \left(\frac{2}{5}\right)^n}{\frac{3}{5}}\text{.} \end{equation*}
  4. If instead of the original geometric sum \(S_n\text{,}\) we had \(a = 7\) and
    \begin{equation*} S_n = 7 + 7 \cdot \frac{2}{5} + 7 \cdot \left(\frac{2}{5}\right)^2 + \cdots + 7 \cdot \left(\frac{2}{5}\right)^{n-1}\text{,} \end{equation*}
    the only thing that would change is that our shortcut formula for \(S_n\) would be multiplied by \(7\text{:}\)
    \begin{equation*} S_n = \frac{7 - 7 \cdot \left(\frac{2}{5}\right)^n}{\frac{3}{5}} \end{equation*}
