Looking at a graph of the region, y starts at \(c=-6\) and ends at \(d=2\text{,}\) and x goes from the line \(x=y\) on the left side of the region to the graph of the sideways parabola \(4x+y^2=12\) on the right. But that has x in it, so we solve for x and get \(x=\frac{12-y^2}{4}\text{.}\) So the integral is \(\displaystyle \int_{-6}^2 \frac{12-y^2}{4}-y \ dy\)
Now we can simplify and integrate,
\begin{equation*} \displaystyle \int_{-6}^2 \frac{12-y^2}{4}-y \ dy =\int_{-6}^2 \left[ 3-\frac{1}{4}y^2-y \right] \ dy = 3y- \frac{1}{12}y^3-\frac{1}{2}y^2 \biggr|_{-6}^2 \end{equation*}
\begin{equation*} =6-\frac{8}{12}-\frac{4}{2}-\left( -18+\frac{6^3}{12}-\frac{36}{2} \right) = \frac{64}{3}=21.3333 \end{equation*}
