\(1/v\) is undefined at \(v=0\text{.}\) To evaluate it, we must split the region of integration up into two pieces, from 0 to 4 and from \(-4\) to 0. But notice,
\begin{equation*} \int_0^{4}{1\over v}\, dv = \lim_{b\to 0^+}\int_b^{4} {1\over v}\, dv = \lim_{b\to 0^+}\left({\ln v}\bigg|_b^{4}\right) = \ln(4) - \ln b. \end{equation*}
As \(b\to 0^+\text{,}\) this goes to infinity and the integral diverges, so our original integral also diverges.
