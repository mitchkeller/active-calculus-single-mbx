a. We were given that
\begin{equation*} A(x) = \int_1^x f(t) \, dt, \end{equation*}
so \(A(1) = \int_1^1 f(t) \, dt =0.\)
Then \(A(2) = \int_1^2 f(t) \, dt = \int_1^2 (4-2t)\, dt = 4t-t^2 \bigg\vert_1^2 = (8-4)-(4-1) = 1\)
b. \(A(x) = \int_1^x f(t) \, dt = \int_1^x (4-2t)\, dt = 4t-t^2 \bigg\vert_1^x = (4x-x^2)-(4-1) = 4x-x^{2}-3\)
c. \(4x-x^{2}-3\) is a quadratic function.
d. The derivative of \(4x-x^{2}-3\) is \(4-2x\text{.}\)
e. Thus, the derivative of \(A\) is \(f\text{,}\) or \(A'=f\text{,}\) so \(A\) is an antiderivative of \(f\text{.}\)
