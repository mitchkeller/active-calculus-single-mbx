Solution 9.6.7.1.

\(r=7 - \cos \theta\) in \(r\) and \(\theta\) (cartesian, not polar, this time):
\(r_0=6 ,\quad r_1=7 \quad r_2=8\) We see that as \(\theta\) varies from \(0\) to \(\pi\text{,}\) the radius \(r\) increases from \(6\) to \(8\text{,}\) so we get the upper half of the cardioid (the lower half is obtained as \(\theta\) varies from \(\pi\) to \(2\pi\) and consequently \(r\) decreases from \(8\) to \(6\text{.}\) Since the cardioid is symmetric with respect to the \(x\)-axis we may compute the upper area and double the result. Using
\begin{equation*} \cos^{2}\theta =\frac{ \cos 2\theta +1}{2} \end{equation*}
we get
\begin{equation*} A = 2\cdot \frac{1}{2}\int_{0}^{\pi }{r}^{2} \,d\theta= \int_{0}^{\pi }{\left(7- \cos \theta \right)}^{2} \,d\theta= \int_{0}^{\pi}\left(49-14 \cos \theta +\cos^{2}\theta \right) \,d\theta= \int_{0}^{\pi}\left(49-14 \cos \theta +\frac{ \cos 2\theta +1}{2}\right)\,d\theta= \end{equation*}
\begin{equation*} \int_{0}^{\pi }\left(\frac{99}{2}-14 \cos \theta +\frac{1}{2} \cos 2\theta \right) \,d\theta = \frac{99}{2}\theta -14 \sin \theta +\frac{1}{4} \sin 2\theta \bigg|_{0}^{\pi }= \frac{99\pi }{2} \end{equation*}
