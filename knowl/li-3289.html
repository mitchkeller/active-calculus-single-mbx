Item 5.2.5.5.c.
We know that \(Y(x)\) measures the total amount of sand on the beach at time \(x\text{.}\) Thus, the instantaneous rate at which the total number of cubic yards of sand on the beach is changing at time \(t=4\) is given by \(Y'(4)\text{.}\) We first compute \(Y'(x)\text{.}\) By the Second Fundamental Theorem of Calculus, we have
\begin{equation*} Y'(x) = S(x) - R(x)\text{,} \end{equation*}
and thus \(Y'(4) = S(4) - R(4) \approx -1.90875\) cubic yards per hour.
