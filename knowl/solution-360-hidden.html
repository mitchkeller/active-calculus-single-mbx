Over the interval \([0,\pi]\text{,}\) the graphs \(y = 3\sin x\) and \(y = 8 \cos x\) intersect when \(3\sin x = 8 \cos x\text{,}\) i.e., \(\tan x = \frac{8}{3}\text{,}\) i.e., \(x=\tan^{-1}(\frac{8}{3})=1.21202565652432\text{.}\) Let \(t = \tan^{-1}(\frac{8}{3})\text{.}\) Over the interval \(\left[0, t\right]\text{,}\) the graph of \(y = 3\sin x\) lies below that of \(y = 8 \cos x\text{.}\) Hence, the area between the two curves is \(\int_0^{ t}(8 \cos x-3 \sin x)dx\)
\begin{equation*} \begin{array}{ll} \amp =\left.(8\sin x+3\cos x)\right|_0^{ t} \cr \amp =\left(8\sin t+3\cos t\right)-(8\cdot 0+3\cdot 1)\cr \amp =\left(8\sin t+3\cos t\right)-3 \end{array} \end{equation*}
Since \(t = \tan^{-1}(\frac{8}{3})\text{,}\) we have \(\tan t = \frac{8}{3}\text{.}\) Considering a rightangle triangle with an angle which opposite side has length 8 and adjacent side has length 3, we see that the hypotenues is \(\sqrt{73}\text{.}\) Thus \(\sin t = \frac{8}{\sqrt{73}}\) and \(\cos t = \frac{3}{\sqrt{73}}\text{.}\) \(8\sin t+3\cos t = \frac{8^2}{\sqrt{73}} + \frac{3^2}{\sqrt{73}} = \frac{8^2+3^2}{\sqrt{73}} = \frac{73}{\sqrt{73}}=\sqrt{73}\text{.}\) Thus, \(\int_0^{ t}(8 \cos x-3 \sin x)dx= \sqrt{73}-3.\) Over the interval \(\left[ t,\pi\right]\text{,}\) the graph of \(y = 3\sin x\) lies above that of \(y = 8 \cos x\text{.}\) Hence, the area between the two curves is \(\int^\pi_{ t}(3\sin x - 8 \cos x)dx\)
\begin{equation*} \begin{array}{ll} \amp =-\left.(8\sin x+3\cos x)\right|^\pi_{ t} \cr \amp =3 + \sqrt{73} \end{array} \end{equation*}
Thus the total area is the sum of the above two integrals, which is \(2\sqrt{73}\text{.}\)
