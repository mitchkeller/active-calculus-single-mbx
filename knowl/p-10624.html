If we widen the interval of \(x\)-values to something like \(-2 \lt x \lt 2\text{,}\) we see similar trends, but at the endpoints of the interval, there is a lot of error. For example, \(f(2) - T_2(2) \approx 2.39\text{;}\) even for \(T_4(x)\text{,}\) we have \(f(2) - T_4(2) \approx 0.39\text{.}\)
