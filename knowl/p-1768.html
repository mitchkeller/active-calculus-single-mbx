While the function does not have a jump in its graph at \(a = 1\text{,}\) it is still not the case that \(g\) approaches a single value as \(x\) approaches 1. In particular, due to the infinitely oscillating behavior of \(g\) to the right of \(a = 1\text{,}\) we say that the right-hand limit of \(g\) as \(x \to 1^+\) does not exist, and thus \(\lim_{x \to 1} g(x) \ \text{does not exist} \text{.}\)
