Item 7.4.3.6.d.
Now we want to find the time \(t\) so that \(M(t) = \frac{1}{4}M_0\text{.}\) Similar work to our recent efforts in (c) shows that \(t = \frac{5730\ln(4)}{\ln(2)} \approx 11460\) years. This number makes sense because it takes \(5730\) years for half of the sample to decay, then another \(5730\) years for half to decay again, for a total of \((2)(5730)\) years for the substance to decay to one quarter of its original amount.
