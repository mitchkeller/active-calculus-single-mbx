Solution 9.3.2.2.1.

The denominator factors as \(x^3 + x = x (x^2+1)\text{.}\) Since the denominator factors in the linear term \(x\text{,}\) and in the irreducible quadratic \(x^2+1\text{,}\) the correct form of the partial fraction is:
\begin{equation*} \displaystyle \frac{A}{x}+\frac{Bx+C}{x^2+1} \end{equation*}
Thus the correct answer is \(\displaystyle \frac{A}{x}+\frac{Bx+C}{x^2+1}\text{.}\)
