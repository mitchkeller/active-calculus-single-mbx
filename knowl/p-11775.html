The integral involves \(\tan u\) and \(\sec u\) where the exponent of \(\sec u\) is even and positive. So we rewrite in order to have one copy of \(\sec^2(15 x)\) for the derivative, and use a trigonometric identity to turn the remaining even powers of \(\sec(15 x)\) into an expression of \(\tan^2(15 x)\text{.}\) Then use u-substitution with \(u= \tan(15 x)\text{.}\)
