If \(f(x) = \sum_{k=0}^{\infty} c_k (x-a)^k\) converges for \(|x-a| \lt R\) for a positive real number \(R\text{,}\) then
\begin{equation*} f'(x) = \sum_{k=1}^{\infty} k \cdot c_k (x-a)^{k-1} \end{equation*}
and
\begin{equation*} \int f(x) \, dx = C + \sum_{k=0}^{\infty} \frac{c_k}{k+1} (x-a)^{k+1} \end{equation*}
and both of these series converge if \(|x-a| \lt R\text{.}\)
