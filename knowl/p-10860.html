  1. Multiplying both sides of Equation (8.3.5) by \(r = \frac{2}{5}\text{,}\) we see that
    \begin{equation*} \frac{2}{5} \cdot S_n = \frac{2}{5} + \left(\frac{2}{5}\right)^2 + \left(\frac{2}{5}\right)^3 + \cdots + \left(\frac{2}{5}\right)^{n}\text{.} \end{equation*}
  2. With
    \begin{equation*} S_n = 1 + \frac{2}{5} + \left(\frac{2}{5}\right)^2 + \cdots + \left(\frac{2}{5}\right)^{n-1} \end{equation*}
    and
    \begin{equation*} \frac{2}{5} \cdot S_n = \frac{2}{5} + \left(\frac{2}{5}\right)^2 + \left(\frac{2}{5}\right)^3 + \cdots + \left(\frac{2}{5}\right)^{n}\text{,} \end{equation*}
    it follows that if we subtract the second equation from the first,
    \begin{equation} S_n - \frac{2}{5} \cdot S_n = 1 - \left(\frac{2}{5}\right)^{n}\text{,}\tag{8.3.6} \end{equation}
    since every term in \(S_n\) except \(1\) appears in \(\frac{2}{5} \cdot S_n\text{,}\) and every term in \(\frac{2}{5} \cdot S_n\) except \(\left(\frac{2}{5}\right)^{n}\) appears in \(\frac{2}{5} \cdot S_n\text{.}\)
  3. Factoring the left side of Equation (8.3.6), we see that
    \begin{equation*} S_n(1 - \frac{2}{5}) = 1 - \left(\frac{2}{5}\right)^{n} \end{equation*}
    and dividing both sides by \((1 - \frac{2}{5})\text{,}\) we have
    \begin{equation*} S_n = \frac{1 - \left(\frac{2}{5}\right)^{n}}{1 - \frac{2}{5}}\text{.} \end{equation*}
    This shows that we can find the value of the \(n\)the partial sum directly by using this shortcut formula, rather than having to actually add up \(n\) terms.
  4. If instead of the original geometric sum \(S_n\text{,}\) we had \(a = 7\) and
    \begin{equation*} S_n = 7 + 7 \cdot \frac{2}{5} + 7 \cdot \left(\frac{2}{5}\right)^2 + \cdots + 7 \cdot \left(\frac{2}{5}\right)^{n-1}\text{,} \end{equation*}
    the only thing that would change is that our shortcut formula for \(S_n\) would be multiplied by \(7\text{.}\)
