We next extend the approach in Preview ActivityÂ 8.5.1 to arbitrary functions at arbitrary points. Let \(f\) be a function that has as many derivatives as we need at a point \(x=a\text{.}\) Recall that \(P_1(x)\) is the tangent line to \(f\) at \((a,f(a))\) and is given by the formula
\begin{equation*} P_1(x) = f(a) + f'(a)(x-a)\text{.} \end{equation*}
\(P_1(x)\) is the linear approximation to \(f\) near \(a\) that has the same slope and function value as \(f\) at the point \(x = a\text{.}\)
