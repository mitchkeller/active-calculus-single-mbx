Solution 1.1.4.3.1.

    1. \(\displaystyle \displaystyle{\frac{f(1) - f(0)}{1 - 0} = \frac{8 - 9}{1 - 0} = \frac{-1}{1} = -1}\)

    2. \(\displaystyle \displaystyle{\frac{f(3) - f(1) }{3 - 1} = \frac{0 - 8}{3 - 1} = \frac{-8}{2} = -4}\)

    3. \(\displaystyle \displaystyle{\frac{f(3) - f(0)}{3 - 0} = \frac{0 - 9}{3 - 0} = \frac{-8}{3} = -3}\)

  2. The formulas in parts (i), (ii), and (iii) represent the average rate of change of \(f(x)\) over the intervals \(0 \leq x \leq 1\text{,}\) \(1 \leq x \leq 3\text{,}\) and \(0 \leq x \leq 3\) respectively. Thus line segment D connecting the points \((0, 9)\) and \((1, 8)\) illustrates formula (i), line segment C connecting the points \((1, 8)\) and \((3, 0)\) illustrates formula (ii), and line segment B connecting the points \((0, 9)\) and \((3, 0)\) illustrates formula (iii).
