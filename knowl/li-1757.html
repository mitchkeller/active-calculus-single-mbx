Item a.
Let \(f(x) = \sqrt{e^x + 3}\text{.}\) By the chain rule \(f'(x) = \frac{e^x}{2\sqrt{e^x + 3}}\text{,}\) and thus \(f'(0) = \frac{1}{4}\text{.}\) Note further that \(f(0) = \sqrt{1 + 3} = 2\text{.}\) The tangent line is therefore the line through \((0,2)\) with slope \(1/4\text{,}\) which is
\begin{equation*} y - 2 = \frac{1}{4}(x-0)\text{.} \end{equation*}
