If instead of the original geometric sum \(S_n\text{,}\) we had \(a = 7\) and
\begin{equation*} S_n = 7 + 7 \cdot \frac{2}{5} + 7 \cdot \left(\frac{2}{5}\right)^2 + \cdots + 7 \cdot \left(\frac{2}{5}\right)^{n-1}\text{,} \end{equation*}
the only thing that would change is that our shortcut formula for \(S_n\) would be multiplied by \(7\text{.}\)
