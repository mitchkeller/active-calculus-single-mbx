We use the ratio test:
\begin{equation*} \left|{a_{n+1} \over {a_n}}\right| = \left|{{(n+1)^4 x^{4(n+1)}} \over {3^{4(n+1)}}} \cdot {{3^{4 n}} \over {n^4 x^{4 n}}}\right| = \left({{n+1} \over n}\right)^2 \cdot {{x^4} \over 81}. \end{equation*}
Since \({{(n+1)}/ n} \rightarrow 1\) as \(n \rightarrow \infty\text{,}\) we have
\begin{equation*} \lim_{n\to\infty} \left|{{a_{n+1}} \over {a_n}}\right|={{x^4}\over 81}. \end{equation*}
We have \({{x^4}/ 81} \lt 1\) when \(|x| \lt 3\text{.}\) The radius of convergence is 3 and the series converges for \(-3 \lt x \lt 3\text{.}\) (And we donâ€™t know what happens at \(x=2\) or \(x=-2\text{.}\))
