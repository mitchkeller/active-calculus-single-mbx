To use twice as many subintervals, we move to \(\triangle x = 0.2\) and thus \(x_1 = 1.2\text{,}\) \(x_2 = 1.4\text{,}\) and so on, with the general right endpoint being given by \(x_i = 1 + 0.2 \cdot i\text{.}\) We'll also have \(n = 10\text{,}\) so

\begin{equation*} R_{10} = \sum_{i=1}^{10} \left( (1+0.2i)^2 + 1 \right) \cdot 0.2\text{.} \end{equation*}
