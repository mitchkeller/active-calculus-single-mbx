Solution 5.4.7.1.1.

Let \(u=x\) and \(dv=\cos\mathopen{}\left(3x\right)dx\text{.}\)
Then \(du=dx\) and \(v=\frac{1}{3} \sin(3 x)\text{.}\)
The integration by parts formula says that
\begin{equation*} \int u \, dv = u\cdot v - \int v \, du \end{equation*}
So, substituting in, we get that
\begin{equation*} \int x \cos(3 x) \, dx = x \cdot \frac{1}{3}\sin(3 x - \int \frac{1}{3}\sin(3 x) \cdot dx \end{equation*}
Lastly, we integrate the second part, so the answer is
\begin{equation*} \int x \cos(3 x) \, dx = \frac{1}{3} x \sin(3 x) + \frac{1}{9}\cos(3 x)+C \end{equation*}
