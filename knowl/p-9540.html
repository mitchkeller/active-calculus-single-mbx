In EquationÂ (8.3.12), we learned that for an infinite geometric series
\begin{equation*} S = a + ar + ar^n + \cdots + ar^{n-1} + \cdots\text{,} \end{equation*}
if \(|r| \lt 1\text{,}\) then
\begin{equation*} S = \frac{a}{1-r}\text{.} \end{equation*}
This result provides a quick way to determine the value of an infinite geometric series that converges.
