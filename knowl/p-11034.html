In the sum \(2 + \frac{8}{3} + \frac{32}{9} + \frac{128}{27} + \cdots \text{,}\) we notice that to get from one term to the next, say from \(\frac{8}{3}\) to \(\frac{32}{9}\text{,}\) we have to multiply by \(r = \frac{4}{3}\text{.}\) Thus, the geometric sum has \(a = 2\) and \(r = \frac{4}{3}\text{.}\) Since \(|r| = \frac{4}{3} \ge 1\text{,}\) the infinite series diverges, but we can still find the partial sums \(S_5\) and \(S_{10}\text{:}\)
\begin{equation*} S_5 = \frac{a - ar^5}{1-r} = \frac{2 - 2\cdot(4/3)^5}{1-(4/3)} = \frac{1562}{81} \approx 19.284 \end{equation*}
and
\begin{equation*} S_{10} = \frac{a - ar^{10}}{1-r} = \frac{2 - 2\cdot(4/3)^{10}}{1-(4/3)} = \frac{1979054}{19683} \approx 100.546\text{.} \end{equation*}
