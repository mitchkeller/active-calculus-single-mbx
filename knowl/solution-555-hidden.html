The integral involves \(\cot u\) and \(\csc u\) where the exponent of \(\cot u\) is odd and positive. So we rewrite in order to have a copy of \(\csc(4 x) \cot(4 x)\) and use a trigonometric identity to turn the remaining even powers of \(\cot(4 x)\) into an expression of \(\csc^2(4 x)\text{.}\)
Then let \(u= \csc(4 x)\text{,}\) which means that \(du= -4 \csc(4 x)\cot(4 x) \, dx\text{,}\) and thus \(dx= \frac{du}{-4\csc(4 x)\cot(4 x)}\text{.}\)
\begin{equation*} \begin{aligned} \int 5\cot^{3}\!\left(4x\right)\csc^{3}\!\left(4x\right) \, dx \amp = 5 \int \cot^2(4 x) \csc^2(4 x) \csc(4 x) \cot(4 x) \, dx \\\\ \amp = 5 \int \bigg( \csc^2(4 x)-1 \bigg) \csc^2(4 x) \csc(4 x) \cot(4 x) \, dx \\\\ \amp = 5 \int \bigg( u^2-1 \bigg) u^2 \csc(4 x) \cot(4 x) \frac{du}{-4\csc(4 x)\cot(4 x)} \\\\ \amp = \frac{5}{-4} \int \bigg( u^2-1 \bigg) u^2 \, du \\\\ \amp = \frac{5}{-4} \int \bigg( u^4-u^2 \bigg) \, du \\\\ \amp = \frac{5}{-4} \bigg[\frac{ u^5}{5}-\frac{u^3}{3}\bigg]+C \\\\ \amp = \frac{5}{-4} \bigg[\frac{ \csc^5(4 x)}{5}-\frac{\csc^3(4 x)}{3}\bigg]+C \end{aligned} \end{equation*}
The correct trigonometric identity used is E.
