Item 7.2.4.8.c.

For positive \(y\) near \(0\text{,}\) \(M(y) = \frac{y}{2+y} \approx 0\text{;}\) for large values of \(y\text{,}\) \(M(y) = \frac{y}{2+y} \approx 1\text{.}\) This means that a single cat can remove about \(1\) thousand mice per year from the population when the mice population is large.
