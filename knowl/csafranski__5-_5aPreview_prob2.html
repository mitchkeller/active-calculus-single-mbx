Question 9.3.2.

First, note that we can use algebra to combine \(\frac{-8}{x+3}+\frac{2}{x-3}\) into one fraction by finding a common denominator.
\begin{equation*} \frac{-8}{x+3}+\frac{2}{x-3} = \frac{-8}{x+3}\cdot\frac{x-3}{x-3}+\frac{2}{x-3}\cdot \frac{x+3}{x+3}=\frac{30-6x}{x^{2}-9} \end{equation*}
Also note that we know how to integrate
\(\displaystyle \int \frac{-8}{x+3} \, dx =\) and
\(\displaystyle \int \frac{2}{x-3} \, dx =\)
Which means we can integrate both sides of \(\frac{-8}{x+3}+\frac{2}{x-3} = \frac{30-6x}{x^{2}-9}\) to get
\(\displaystyle \int \frac{30-6x}{x^{2}-9} \, dx =\)
