Let \(A(t)\) be the amount of money (in millions) in the account at time \(t\text{.}\) We know that the rate of change of \(A\) is affected by the addition of \(1\) million dollars a year, plus the interest earned, which is \(0.05A\text{.}\) Thus,
\begin{equation*} \frac{dA}{dt} = 1 + 0.05A \end{equation*}
