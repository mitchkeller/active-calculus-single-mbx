The function \(\displaystyle y=\frac{x^{3}}{\sqrt{x}+10x^{9}}\) is a quotient of two functions, say \(f(x)= x^{3}\) and \(g(x)=\sqrt{x}+10x^{9}\text{.}\) Then taking the derivatives we get \(f'(x)=3x^{2}\) and \(g'(x)=\frac{1}{2\sqrt{x}}+10\cdot 9x^{8}\text{.}\) Finally, the quotient rule says that
\begin{equation*} \left[\frac{f(x)}{g(x)}\right]'=\frac{g(x)f'(x)-f(x)g'(x)}{\left[g(x)\right]^2} \end{equation*}
so
\begin{equation*} y'= \frac{(\sqrt{x}+10x^{9}) \cdot (3x^{2}) - (x^{3})\cdot \frac{1}{2\sqrt{x}}+10\cdot 9x^{8}}{(\sqrt{x}+10x^{9})^2}. \end{equation*}
