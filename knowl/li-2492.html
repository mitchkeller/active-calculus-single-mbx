Item c.
With \(q(x) = \frac{x^2}{x-2}\text{,}\) we have
\begin{equation*} q'(x) = \frac{(x-2)(2x) - x^2(1)}{(x-2)^2} = \frac{2x^2 - 4x - x^2}{(x-2)^2} = \frac{x^2-4x}{(x-2)^2} = \frac{x(x-4)}{(x-2)^2}\text{.} \end{equation*}
Hence, the critical numbers of \(q\) are \(x = 0\) and \(x = 4\text{.}\) Only the latter critical number lies in the interval \([3,7]\text{,}\) and thus we evaluate \(q\) and find
  • \(\displaystyle q(4) = \frac{16}{2} = 8\)
  • \(\displaystyle q(3) = \frac{9}{1} = 9\)
  • \(\displaystyle q(7) = \frac{49}{5} = 9.8\)
We now see that on \([3,7]\) the absolute maximum of \(q\) is 9.8 and the absolute minimum is 8.
