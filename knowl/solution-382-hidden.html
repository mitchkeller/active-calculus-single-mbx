(a) Suppose we choose an \(x\text{,}\) \(0\leq x\leq 5\text{.}\) If \(\Delta x\) is a small fraction of a meter, then the density of the rod is approximately \(\delta(x)\) anywhere from \(x\) to \(x+\Delta x\) meters from the left end of the rod. The mass of the rod from \(x\) to \(x+\Delta x\) meters is therefore approximately \(\delta(x) \Delta x = (4 + 3 x)\Delta x\text{.}\) If we slice the rod into \(N\) pieces, then a Riemann sum is \(\Sigma_{i=1}^{N}(4 + 3 x_i)\Delta x\text{,}\) or, more loosely, \(\Sigma (4 + 3 x)\Delta x\text{.}\) (b) The definite integral is
\begin{equation*} M=\int_0^{5} \delta(x)\,dx = \int_0^{5} (4 + 3 x)\,dx= (4 x + {3\over2} x^2)\bigg|_0^{5} = {115\over2} \hbox{ grams}. \end{equation*}
