The integration by parts formula says that
\begin{equation*} \int u \, dv = u\cdot v - \int v \, du \end{equation*}
So, substituting in, we get that
\begin{equation*} \int x \sec^{2}\!\left(5x\right) \, dx = x \cdot \frac{1}{5} \tan(5 x) - \int \frac{1}{5} \tan(5 x) \, dx \end{equation*}
\begin{equation*} =\frac{1}{5} x \tan(5 x) - \int \frac{1}{5} \tan(5 x) \, dx \end{equation*}
Unfortunately, the integral is not Basic, but we can rewrite it and integrate it using u-substitution.
\begin{equation*} \frac{1}{5} x \tan(5 x) - \int \frac{1}{5} \frac{\sin(5 x)}{\cos(5 x)} \, dx \end{equation*}
Let \(u=\cos(5 x)\) so \(du = -5 \sin(5 x) \, dx\) and solving for \(dx\) gives us \(dx=\frac{du}{-5 \sin(5 x)}\text{.}\)
