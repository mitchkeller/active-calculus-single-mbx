Item 5.4.7.5.b.

To use the First Fundamental Theorem of Calculus to evaluate the integral in \(F(x) = \int_0^x te^{-2t} \, dt\text{,}\) we use integration by parts. Letting

\begin{align*} u &= t, \ du = dt\\ dv &= e^{-2t} \, dt, \ v = -\frac{1}{2}e^{-2t}\text{,} \end{align*}

it follows that

\begin{equation*} \int te^{-2t} \, dt = -\frac{1}{2}te^{-2t} - \int \left( -\frac{1}{2}e^{-2t} \right) \, dt = -\frac{1}{2}te^{-2t} - \frac{1}{4}e^{-2t}\text{.} \end{equation*}

Thus,

\begin{align*} F(x) &= \int_0^x te^{-2t} \, dt\\ &= \left. -\frac{1}{2}te^{-2t} - \frac{1}{4}e^{-2t} \right|_{0}^{x}\\ &= -\frac{1}{2}xe^{-2x} - \frac{1}{4}e^{-2x} - \left( 0 - \frac{1}{4}e^{0} \right)\\ &= -\frac{1}{2}xe^{-2x} - \frac{1}{4}e^{-2x} + \frac{1}{4} \end{align*}
