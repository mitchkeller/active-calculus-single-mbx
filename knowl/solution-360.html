Solution 6.1.5.2.1.

Over the interval \([0,\pi]\text{,}\) the graphs \(y = 5\sin x\) and \(y = 10 \cos x\) intersect when \(5\sin x = 10 \cos x\text{,}\) i.e., \(\tan x = \frac{10}{5}\text{,}\) i.e., \(x=\tan^{-1}(\frac{10}{5})=1.10714871779409\text{.}\) Let \(t = \tan^{-1}(\frac{10}{5})\text{.}\) Over the interval \(\left[0, t\right]\text{,}\) the graph of \(y = 5\sin x\) lies below that of \(y = 10 \cos x\text{.}\) Hence, the area between the two curves is \(\int_0^{ t}(10 \cos x-5 \sin x)dx\)
\begin{equation*} \begin{array}{ll} \amp =\left.(10\sin x+5\cos x)\right|_0^{ t} \cr \amp =\left(10\sin t+5\cos t\right)-(10\cdot 0+5\cdot 1)\cr \amp =\left(10\sin t+5\cos t\right)-5 \end{array} \end{equation*}
Since \(t = \tan^{-1}(\frac{10}{5})\text{,}\) we have \(\tan t = \frac{10}{5}\text{.}\) Considering a rightangle triangle with an angle which opposite side has length 10 and adjacent side has length 5, we see that the hypotenues is \(\sqrt{125}\text{.}\) Thus \(\sin t = \frac{10}{\sqrt{125}}\) and \(\cos t = \frac{5}{\sqrt{125}}\text{.}\) \(10\sin t+5\cos t = \frac{10^2}{\sqrt{125}} + \frac{5^2}{\sqrt{125}} = \frac{10^2+5^2}{\sqrt{125}} = \frac{125}{\sqrt{125}}=\sqrt{125}\text{.}\) Thus, \(\int_0^{ t}(10 \cos x-5 \sin x)dx= \sqrt{125}-5.\) Over the interval \(\left[ t,\pi\right]\text{,}\) the graph of \(y = 5\sin x\) lies above that of \(y = 10 \cos x\text{.}\) Hence, the area between the two curves is \(\int^\pi_{ t}(5\sin x - 10 \cos x)dx\)
\begin{equation*} \begin{array}{ll} \amp =-\left.(10\sin x+5\cos x)\right|^\pi_{ t} \cr \amp =5 + \sqrt{125} \end{array} \end{equation*}
Thus the total area is the sum of the above two integrals, which is \(2\sqrt{125}\text{.}\)
