Item 5.4.7.14.a.
We first write
\begin{equation*} \int e^{2x} \cos(e^x) \, dx = \int e^x \cdot e^x \cos(e^x) \, dx\text{.} \end{equation*}
Letting \(z = e^x\text{,}\) we have \(dz = e^x \, dx\text{,}\) and thus
\begin{equation*} \int e^{2x} \cos(e^x) \, dx = \int z \cos(z) \, dz\text{.} \end{equation*}
