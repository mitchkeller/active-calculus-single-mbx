The population will be 12 billion when \(P(t) = 12\text{.}\) So we solve the equation \(6.084e^{0.01241t} = 12\) for \(t\text{.}\) We see that \(e^{0.01241t} = \frac{12}{6.084}\text{,}\) so \(t = \frac{1}{0.01241} \ln \left( \frac{12}{6.084}\right) \approx 56.41\text{.}\) The population will reach 12 billion during the year 2056.
