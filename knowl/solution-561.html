Solution 9.4.10.1.

\(t = \sqrt {2}\) and \(t = -\sqrt {2}\) yields the same x and y coordinates, namely (0,14). These two values of t are going to be the upper and lower limits of integration. The area under a curve defined by parameters \(x = f(t)\) and \(y = g(t)\) can be expressed
\begin{equation*} A = \int ydx =\int g(t)f'(t)dt \end{equation*}
Plugging in the functions and the upper and lower limits of integration, we have
\begin{equation*} A = \int_{-\sqrt{2}}^{\sqrt{2}} 7 t^2 (3 t^2 - 2) dt \end{equation*}
\begin{equation*} = (2) (7) \int_{0}^{\sqrt{2}} (3 t^4 - 2 t^2) dt \end{equation*}
\begin{equation*} = 14 \left[ \frac {3}{5} t^5 - \frac {2}{3} t^3 \right]_{0}^{\sqrt{2}} \end{equation*}
\begin{equation*} = 14 \left[ \frac {3}{5} 2^{5/2} - \frac {2}{3} 2^{3/2} \right] \end{equation*}
\begin{equation*} = 21.1189225314382 \end{equation*}
