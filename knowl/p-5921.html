Substituting back into the integral gives us:
\begin{equation*} \int \frac{1}{5} \frac{\sin(5 x)}{\cos(5 x)} \, dx = \int \frac{1}{5} \frac{\sin(5 x)}{u} \, \frac{du}{-5 \sin(5 x)} \end{equation*}
\begin{equation*} = \int \frac{-1}{25} \frac{du}{u} \end{equation*}
This integrates to \(\frac{-1}{25} \ln(u)\) and substituting back in for \(u\) gives
\begin{equation*} \frac{-1}{25} \ln(\cos(5 x))+C \end{equation*}
Combining it with the non-integrated term from integration by parts, we get the final answer:
\begin{equation*} \int x \sec^{2}\!\left(5x\right) \, dx = \frac{1}{5} x \tan(5 x) - \frac{-1}{25} \ln(\cos(5 x))+C \end{equation*}
