To begin finding \(T_n(x)\text{,}\) do the usual work of computing the various derivatives of \(f\) and their respective values at \(a = 0\text{;}\) note that it's helpful to view \(f(x)\) in the form \(f(x) = (1-x)^{-1}\) so that we can easily compute the derivatives of \(f\) using the chain rule. For instance,
\begin{equation*} f'(x) = (-1)(1-x)^{-2}(-1) \end{equation*}
where the first “\(-1\)” arises from the power rule, while the second “\(-1\)” results from the chain rule, since \(\frac{d}{dx}[1-x] = -1\text{.}\) In order to see key patterns that arise, it's helpful not to combine the products of numbers that arise in the various derivatives.
