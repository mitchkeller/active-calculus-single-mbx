\begin{equation*} \frac{2x}{x^2-1}=\frac{A}{x+1}+\frac{B}{x-1} \end{equation*}
so that
\begin{equation*} 2x = A(x-1)+B(x+1) = (A+B)x + 1 (-A+B). \end{equation*}
Solving, we get \(A=B=1\text{,}\) so that
\begin{equation*} \int \frac{2x}{x^2-1}\,dx= \int \left(\frac{1}{x+1}+\frac{1}{x-1}\right)\,dx =\ln|x+1|+\ln|x-1|+C. \end{equation*}
Using the substitution \(w=x^2-1\text{,}\) we get \(dw= 2x\,dx\text{,}\) so we have
\begin{equation*} \int \frac{2x}{x^2-1}\,dx= \int \frac{dw}{w}=\ln|w|+C = \ln|x^2-1|+C. \end{equation*}
The properties of logarithms show that the two results are the same:
\begin{equation*} \ln|x+1|+\ln|x-1| = \ln|(x+1)(x-1)| =\ln|x^2-1|. \end{equation*}
