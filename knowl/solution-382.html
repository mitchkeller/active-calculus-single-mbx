Solution 5.5.5.2.1.

\begin{equation*} \frac{1}{(x+6)(x+3)}=\frac{A}{x+6}+\frac{B}{x+3}, \end{equation*}
giving
\begin{equation*} 1 = A(x+3)+B(x+6), \end{equation*}
or
\begin{equation*} 1 = (A+B)x+ (3 A+6 B). \end{equation*}
Solving for \(A\) and \(B\text{,}\) we find \(A = -0.333333\) and \(B = 0.333333\text{,}\) so
\begin{equation*} \int \frac{1}{(x+6)(x+3)}\,dx= \int\frac{-0.333333}{x+6}\,dx + \int \frac{0.333333}{x+3}\,dx = 0.333333\ln\mathopen{}\left(\left|x+3\right|\right)-0.333333\ln\mathopen{}\left(\left|x+6\right|\right)+C. \end{equation*}
