Solution 8.3.5.2.1.

A. The series \(4 + 4 (0.5) + 4 (0.5)^2 + \cdots + 4 (0.5)^{12}\) has 13 terms, and its sum is
\begin{equation*} 4 + 4 (0.5) + 4 (0.5)^2 + \cdots + 4 (0.5)^{12} = {4 (1 - 0.5^{13})\over 1 - 0.5}. \end{equation*}
B. The series \(4 (0.5)^{6} + 4 (0.5)^{7} + 4 (0.5)^{8} + \cdots + 4 (0.5)^{11}\) has \(11 - 6 + 1 = 6\) terms. We can write it as
\begin{equation*} 4 (0.5)^{6} + 4 (0.5)^{7} + 4 (0.5)^{8} + \cdots + 4 (0.5)^{11} = (4 (0.5)^{6}) (1 + 0.5 + \cdots + 0.5^{5}), \end{equation*}
so its sum is
\begin{equation*} (4 (0.5)^{6}) (1 + 0.5 + \cdots + 0.5^{5}) = {4 (0.5)^{6} (1 - 0.5^{6})\over 1 - 0.5}. \end{equation*}
