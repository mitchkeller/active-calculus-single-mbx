We differentiate implicitly both sides of the equation with respect to \(x\text{.}\)
\begin{equation*} \ln y + \frac xy \, \frac{dy}{dx} + 4 y^{3}\frac{dy}{dx} = \frac{8}{x}, \end{equation*}
so
\begin{equation*} \left( \frac{x}{y} + 4 y^{3} \right) \, \frac{dy}{dx} = \frac{8}{x} - \ln y, \end{equation*}
and thus
\begin{equation*} \frac{dy}{dx} = \frac{\frac{8}{x} - \ln y}{\frac{x}{y} + 4 y^{3}} = \frac{y\mathopen{}\left(8-x\ln\mathopen{}\left(y\right)\right)}{x\mathopen{}\left(x+4y^{4}\right)}. \end{equation*}
