Since \(g'(x) = \frac{(x+4)(x-1)^2}{x-2}\text{,}\) we see that \(g'(x) = 0\) implies that \(x = -4\) or \(x = 1\text{.}\) While \(x = 2\) makes \(g'\) undefined, we are told that \(g\) has a vertical asymptote at \(x = 2\text{,}\) so \(x = 2\) is not in the domain of \(g\text{,}\) and hence is technically not a critical number of \(g\text{.}\) Nonetheless, we place \(x = 2\) on our first derivative sign chart since the vertical asymptote is a location at which \(g'\) may change sign.
