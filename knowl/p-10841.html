At such a value \(a\text{,}\) the degree \(n\) Taylor polynomial of \(f\) centered at \(a\) has form
\begin{equation*} T_n(x) = c_0 + c_1 (x-a) + c_2 (x-a)^2 + \cdots + c_n (x-a)^n \end{equation*}
and it follows that the coefficients \(c_k\) are determined by the values of the various derivatives of \(f(x)\) evaluated at \(a\) according to the formula
\begin{equation*} c_k = \frac{f^{(k)}(a)}{k!}. \end{equation*}
Moreover, for \(x\) near \(a\text{,}\)
\begin{equation*} f(x) \approx T_n(x) = f(a) + f'(a) (x-a) + \frac{f''(a)}{2!} (x-a)^2 + \cdots + \frac{f^{(n)}(a)}{n!} (x-a)^n\text{.} \end{equation*}
