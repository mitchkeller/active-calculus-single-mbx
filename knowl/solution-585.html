Solution 9.4.2.1.

The integral involves \(\sin u\) and \(\cos u\) where the exponents of both are even, non-negative integers (namely 2 for one of them and 0 for the other), and thus the answer to the first multiple choice is C. This means we use the appropriate half-angle identity, which is A.
Thus the integral can be rewritten as
\begin{equation*} \int -7\sin^{2}\mathopen{}\left(6x\right) \, dx = -7 \int \frac{ 1 - \cos(12 x)}{2} dx \end{equation*}
And it could be calculated by continuing on:
Pulling out the constant turns the integral into two different, basic integrals.
\begin{equation*} \frac{-7}{2} \int \left( 1 - \cos(12 x) \right) \,dx = \frac{-7}{2} \int dx - \frac{-7}{2} \int \cos(12 x) \, dx \end{equation*}
So this integrates to
\begin{equation*} \frac{-7 x}{2} - \frac{-7 \sin(12 x)}{24} +C \end{equation*}
