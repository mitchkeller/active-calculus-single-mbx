Solution 9.4.1.1.

We factor out a copy of \(\cos(5 x)\) and use the identity \(\sin^2 u + \cos^2 u = 1\) to turn the remaining \(\cos^2(5 x)\) into an expression involving \(\sin^2(5 x)\text{.}\)
Then we use u-substitution, letting \(u= \sin (5 x)\) so \(du = 5 \cos (5 x) \, dx\) and thus \(\displaystyle dx = \frac{ du}{5 \cos (5 x)}\text{.}\)
\begin{equation*} \begin{aligned} \int \sin^{2}\mathopen{}\left(5x\right)\cos^{3}\mathopen{}\left(5x\right) \, dx \amp = \int \sin^{2} (5 x) \cos^2(5 x) \cos(5 x) \, dx \\\\ \amp = \int \sin^{2} (5 x) \bigg(1-\sin^2(5 x)\bigg) \cos(5 x) \, dx \\\\ \amp = \int u^{2} (1-u^2) \cos(5 x) \, \frac{ du}{5 \cos (5 x)} \\\\ \amp = \frac{1}{5}\int u^{2} (1-u^2) \, du \\\\ \amp = \frac{1}{5}\int u^{2} -u^{4} \, du \\\\ \amp = \frac{1}{5} \left[ \frac{u^{3}}{3} - \frac{u^{5}}{5} \right]+C \\\\ \amp = \frac{1}{5} \left[ \frac{\sin^{3}(5 x) }{3} - \frac{\sin^{5}(5 x)}{5} \right]+C \\\\ \end{aligned} \end{equation*}
The correct trigonometric identity used is B.
