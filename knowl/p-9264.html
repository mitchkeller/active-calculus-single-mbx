If \(f\) is a function with at least \(n\) derivatives at \(a\text{,}\) then the degree \(n\) Taylor polynomial of \(f\) centered at \(a\text{,}\) \(T_n(x)\text{,}\) is
\begin{equation*} T_n(x) = c_0 + c_1(x-a) + c_2(x-a)^2 + \cdots + c_n(x-a)^n \end{equation*}
where each coefficient \(c_k\) is given by
\begin{equation*} c_k = \frac{f^{(k)}(a)}{k!}\text{.} \end{equation*}
