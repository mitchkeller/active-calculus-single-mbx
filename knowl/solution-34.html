Solution 1.3.3.6.1.

\(B=(4.4,2.6)\) is on the graph of \(g\text{,}\) we have \(g(4.4)=2.6\text{.}\) The slope of the tangent line touching the graph at \(x=2\) is given by
\begin{equation*} \mbox{slope} = \frac{\mbox{rise}}{\mbox{run}} = \frac{2.6-2.53}{4.4-4.36} = 1.75. \end{equation*}
Thus, \(g'(4.4)=1.75\text{.}\)
