Solution 5.4.7.1.1.

Let \(u=x\) and \(dv=\cos\!\left(4x\right)dx\text{.}\)
Then \(du=dx\) and \(v=\frac{1}{4} \sin(4 x)\text{.}\)
The integration by parts formula says that
\begin{equation*} \int u \, dv = u\cdot v - \int v \, du \end{equation*}
So, substituting in, we get that
\begin{equation*} \int x \cos(4 x) \, dx = x \cdot \frac{1}{4}\sin(4 x - \int \frac{1}{4}\sin(4 x) \cdot dx \end{equation*}
Lastly, we integrate the second part, so the answer is
\begin{equation*} \int x \cos(4 x) \, dx = \frac{1}{4} x \sin(4 x) + \frac{1}{16}\cos(4 x)+C \end{equation*}
