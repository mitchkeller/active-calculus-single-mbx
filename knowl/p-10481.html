Early in our study of calculus in SectionÂ 1.8, we learned that if a function \(f\) has a derivative at a fixed value \(x=a\text{,}\) when we zoom in on its graph near \((a,f(a))\text{,}\) the function looks linear. Indeed, such a function is differentiable, and we know that near a fixed input value \(a\text{,}\)
\begin{equation*} f(x) \approx L(x) = f(a) + f'(a)(x-a), \end{equation*}
where \(L\) is the tangent line approximation to \(f\) at \(a\text{.}\)
