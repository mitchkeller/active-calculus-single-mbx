Solution 6.1.5.9.1.

Since \(y=\frac14x^4\text{,}\)
\begin{equation*} 1+(y')^2=1+(x^3)^2=1+x^6 \end{equation*}
Therefore \(\sqrt{1+y'^2}=\sqrt{1+x^6}\) and the arc length over \([1,2]\) is:
\begin{equation*} \int_1^2\sqrt{1+x^6}dx \end{equation*}
Let \(f(x)=\sqrt{1+x^6}\text{.}\) We take the estimate of the integral using the Trapezoidal Rule with 9 subdivisions. Since \(n=9\text{,}\)
\begin{equation*} \Delta x=\frac{2-1}{9} \end{equation*}
\begin{equation*} x_i=1+i\Delta x\:(i=0,\ldots,9) \end{equation*}
\begin{equation*} y_i=f(1+i\Delta x) \end{equation*}
\begin{equation*} T_{9}=\sum_{i=0}^{8}\frac12(y_i+y_{i+1})\Delta x \end{equation*}
