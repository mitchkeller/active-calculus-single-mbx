When identifying variables and drawing a picture, it is important to think about the dynamic ways in which the quantities change. Sometimes a sequence of pictures can be helpful; for some pictures that can be easily modified as applets built in Geogebra, see the following links, 1  which represent

  • how a circular oil slick's area grows 2  as its radius increases;

  • how the location of the base of a ladder and its height along a wall change 3  as the ladder slides;

  • how the water level changes 4  in a conical tank as it fills with water at a constant rate (compare the problem in Activity 3.5.2);

  • how a skateboarder's shadow changes 5  as he moves past a lamppost.
