Item 3.2.3.5.a.
Treating \(m\) and \(s\) as constants, by the chain rule,
\begin{equation*} E'(x) = e^{-\frac{(x-m)^2}{2s^2}} \cdot \frac{d}{dx}\left[ -\frac{(x-m)^2}{2s^2} \right] = e^{-\frac{(x-m)^2}{2s^2}} \cdot \left[ -\frac{2(x-m)}{2s^2} \right] \end{equation*}
Note that \(E'(x)\) is defined for every value of \(x\text{,}\) thus the only critical numbers occur where \(E'(x) = 0\text{.}\) Since \(e\) raised to any power is never zero, the only way that \(E'(x) = 0\) is for \(\left[ -\frac{2(x-m)}{2s^2} \right] = 0\text{,}\) which implies that \(x = m\text{.}\) Thus, \(x = m\) is the only critical number of \(E\text{.}\)
