Solution 8.4.4.5.1.

\(4 \sin(x)\) and its first three derivatives are \(f(x) = 4 \sin(x)\text{,}\) \(f'(x) = 4 \cos(x)\text{,}\) \(f''(x) = -4 \sin(x)\text{,}\) and \(f'''(x) = -4 \cos(x)\text{.}\) Thus, evaluating these at \(x = \pi/4\text{,}\) we get the terms term 0 = \({4\over\sqrt2}\) term 1 = \({4\over\sqrt2}(x - {\pi\over4})\) term 2 = \(-{2\over\sqrt{2}}(x - {\pi\over4})^2\text{,}\) and term 3 = \(-{{2\over3\sqrt2}}(x - {\pi\over4})^3\text{.}\) Thus the series is
\begin{equation*} 4 \sin(x) = {4\over\sqrt2} + {4\over\sqrt2}(x - {\pi\over4}) - {2\over\sqrt{2}}(x - {\pi\over4})^2 - {{2\over3\sqrt2}}(x - {\pi\over4})^3 + \cdots. \end{equation*}
