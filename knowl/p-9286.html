  1. Since \(\sqrt[n]{a_n} \approx r\) for large \(n\text{,}\) it follows \(a_n \approx r^n\) for large \(n\text{.}\)

  2. Because \(a_n \approx r^n\) for large \(n\text{,}\) this means that the series \(\sum a_n\) acts like the series \(\sum r^n\) for sufficiently large \(n\text{.}\) The latter is a geometric series with ratio \(r\) between successive terms. Said differently, \(\frac{a_{n+1}}{a_n} \approx \frac{r^{n+1}}{r^n} = r\text{.}\)

  3. Since the terms in our series are all positive, we must have \(r \gt 0\text{.}\) Now the geometric series with ratio \(r\) converges only for \(r \lt 1\text{,}\)so the series

    \begin{equation*} \sum a_k \end{equation*}

    will converge if

    \begin{equation*} \lim_{n \to \infty} \sqrt[n]{a_n} = r \end{equation*}

    with \(0 \lt r \lt 1\text{.}\)
