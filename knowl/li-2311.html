Item 3.2.3.5.c.
From the given formula for \(E''(x)\) and our knowledge that \(e\) to any power is positive, the only way \(E''(x) = 0\) is for
\begin{equation*} \left(\frac{(x-m)^2 - s^2}{s^4} \right) = 0\text{,} \end{equation*}
which implies that \((x-m)^2 = s^2\text{,}\) and therefore \(x-m = \pm s\text{,}\) or \(x = m \pm s\text{.}\) These are the two values for which \(E''(x) = 0\text{.}\)
