\(x\) and \(y\) as being parametrically equations with parameter \(\theta\text{:}\)
\begin{equation*} x = r\cos(\theta) = 4 \sin(3\theta) \cos(\theta), \ \hbox{and}\ y = r\sin(\theta) = 4 \sin(3\theta) \sin(\theta). \end{equation*}
Then we know that the slope of the tangent line is \({dy\over dx} = {dy/d\theta\over dx/d\theta}\text{.}\) Taking the derivatives, \({dx\over d\theta} = 4(3\cos(3\theta)\cos(\theta) - \sin(3\theta)\sin(\theta))\) and \({dy\over d\theta} = 4(3\cos(3\theta)\sin(\theta) + \sin(3\theta)\cos(\theta))\text{.}\) Thus
\begin{equation*} {dy\over dx} = {3\cos(3\theta)\sin(\theta) + \sin(3\theta)\cos(\theta) \over 3\cos(3\theta)\cos(\theta) - \sin(3\theta)\sin(\theta) }. \end{equation*}
We want this at \(\theta = \pi/6\text{,}\) so that we get \({dy\over dx}\approx -1.732\text{.}\) Then, at \(\theta = \pi/6\text{,}\) \(x(\pi/6) = 4 \sin(3\cdot \pi/6)\cos(\pi/6) \approx 3.464\text{,}\) and \(y(\pi/6) = 4 \sin(3\cdot \pi/6)\sin(\pi/6) \approx 2\text{,}\) so that the tangent line is
\begin{equation*} y \approx 2 + (-1.732)(x - 3.464). \end{equation*}
