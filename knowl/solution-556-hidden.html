\(\displaystyle \frac{2}{x}\) and its first three derivatives are \(f(x) = \displaystyle \frac{2}{x}\text{,}\) \(f'(x) = -{2\over x^2}\text{,}\) \(f''(x) = {4\over x^3}\text{,}\) and \(f'''(x) = -{12\over x^4}\text{.}\) Thus, evaluating these at \(x = 1\text{,}\) we get the terms term 0 = \({2}\) term 1 = \(-{2}(x - 1)\) term 2 = \({2}(x - 1)^2\text{,}\) and term 3 = \(-{2}(x - 1)^3\text{.}\) Thus the series is
\begin{equation*} \displaystyle \frac{2}{x} = {2} - {2}(x - 1) + {2}(x - 1)^2 - {2}(x - 1)^3 + \cdots. \end{equation*}
