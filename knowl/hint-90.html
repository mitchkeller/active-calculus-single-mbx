Hint 5.4.7.2.1.

We pick \(u\) and \(dv\) so that \(dv\) can be integrated, and then (ideally) \(u\) gets simpler.
Both \(x\) and \(\sec^{2}\!\left(5x\right)\) can be easily integrated, so pick them so that \(u\) gets simpler.
Let \(u=x\) so \(du= dx\text{.}\)
Then \(dv =\sec^{2}\!\left(5x\right)dx\text{,}\) and we integrate to get that \(v= \frac{1}{5} \tan(5 x)\)
Remember that even though it's not Basic, you do know how to integrate \(\tan(5 x)\text{.}\) It's number 7 on the u-Substitution practice worksheet we did in class.
