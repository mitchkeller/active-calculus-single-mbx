Solution 9.4.7.1.

\(x = \sin 8 t, \, y = \cos 8 t\text{,}\) hence \(x' = 8 \cos 8 t\) and \(y' = - 8 \sin 8 t\text{.}\) By the formula for the arc length we obtain:
\begin{equation*} S = \int ^\pi _0 \sqrt{x'(t)^2 + y'(t)^2} \, dt = \int ^\pi _0 \sqrt{64 \cos^2 8 t + 64 \sin^2 8 t} \, dt = \int ^\pi _0 \sqrt{64} \, dt = 8 \pi \end{equation*}
