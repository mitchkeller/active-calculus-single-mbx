(a) Since \(x - \frac{x^3}{3!} + \frac{x^5}{5!} - \cdots\) is the Taylor series for \(\sin(x)\text{,}\) we know its sum:
\begin{equation*} x - \frac{x^3}{3!} + \frac{x^5}{5!} - \cdots = \sin(x) = 0.3. \end{equation*}
Solving for \(x\text{,}\) we get \(x = \arcsin(0.3)\text{.}\) (b) Similarly, since \(1 - \frac{x^2}{2!} + \frac{x^4}{4!} - \cdots\) is the Taylor series for \(\cos(x)\text{,}\) we know its sum as well:
\begin{equation*} 1 - \frac{x^2}{2!} + \frac{x^4}{4!} - \cdots = \cos(x) = 0.1. \end{equation*}
Solving for \(x\text{,}\) we get \(x = \arccos(0.1)\text{.}\)
