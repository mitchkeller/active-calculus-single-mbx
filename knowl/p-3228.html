It may seem that ExampleÂ 2.5.2 is too elementary to illustrate how to differentiate a composite fuction. Linear functions are the simplest of all functions, and composing linear functions yields another linear function. While this example does not illustrate the full complexity of a composition of nonlinear functions, at the same time we remember that any differentiable function is locally linear, and thus any function with a derivative behaves like a line when viewed up close. The fact that the derivatives of the linear functions \(f\) and \(g\) are multiplied to find the derivative of their composition turns out to be a key insight.
