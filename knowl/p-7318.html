The integration by parts formula says that
\begin{equation*} \int u \, dv = u\cdot v - \int v \, du \end{equation*}
So, substituting in, we get that
\begin{equation*} \int x^7 \ln(-5 x) \, dx = \ln\mathopen{}\left(-5x\right) \cdot \frac{1}{8}x^{8} - \int \frac{1}{8}x^{8} \cdot \frac{1}{x} \, dx \end{equation*}
\begin{equation*} =\ln\mathopen{}\left(-5x\right) \cdot \frac{1}{8}x^{8} - \int \frac{1}{8}x^{7} \, dx \end{equation*}
Lastly, we integrate the second part, so the answer is
\begin{equation*} \int x^7 \ln(-5 x) \, dx = \frac{1}{8}x^{8} \cdot \ln\mathopen{}\left(-5x\right) - \frac{1}{64}x^{8}+C \end{equation*}
