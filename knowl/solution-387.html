Solution 6.2.5.4.1.

\(y=-5\text{.}\) We slice it into rings vertically. A typical ring has thickness \(\Delta x\text{,}\) outer radius \(1+5=6\text{,}\) and inner radius \(y+5=x^{6}+5\text{.}\) Thus
\begin{equation*} \hbox{Volume of slice }\approx \pi 6^2\Delta x - \pi(x^{6}+5)^2\Delta x = \pi(11 - x^{12} - 10 x^{6})\, \Delta x. \end{equation*}
Therefore, we have
\begin{equation*} \hbox{Volume of solid } = \int_0^1\pi(11 - x^{12} - 10 x^{6})\,dx \end{equation*}
\begin{equation*} = \pi \left(11 x - \frac{x^{13}}{13} - {10\over 7} x^{7}\right)\Bigg|_0^1 = \pi ( 11 - {1\over 13} - {10\over 7} ). \end{equation*}
