Solution 3.5.3.6.1.

\(dR/dt=0.6\) when \(R=5\) and \(V=9\) and we want to know \(dI/dt\text{.}\) Differentiating \(I=V/R\) with \(V\) constant gives
\begin{equation*} \frac{dI}{dt}=V\left(-\frac{1}{R^2}\frac{dR}{dt}\right), \end{equation*}
so substituting gives
\begin{equation*} \frac{dI}{dt}=13\left(-\frac{1}{6^2}\cdot 0.6\right) \approx -0.2167 \hbox{ amp/s }. \end{equation*}
(Note that we know the units are amp/s from the Leibnitz form of the derivative, \(dI\over dt\text{.}\) It is, of course, equally correct to use the expression on the right-hand side to determine the units, \(\left(\frac{\hbox{V}}{\hbox{ohm}{}^2}\right)\left(\frac{\hbox{ohm}}{\hbox{s}}\right)\text{,}\) or, in homework-friendly form, V/ohm*s.)
