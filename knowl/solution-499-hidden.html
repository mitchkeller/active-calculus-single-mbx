\begin{equation*} g(x) = g(3) + g'(3)(x-3) + \frac{g''(3)}{2!}(x-3)^2 +\frac{g'''(3)}{3!}(x-3)^3+\cdots \end{equation*}
Substituting gives
\begin{equation*} g(x) = 5 - 3(x-3) + \frac{3}{2!}(x-3)^2 +\frac{-1}{3!}(x-3)^3+\cdots \end{equation*}
(a) The degree 2 Taylor polynomial, \(T_2 (x)\text{,}\) is obtained by truncating after the \((x-3)^2\) term:
\begin{equation*} T_2(x) = 5+\left(-3\right)\!\left(x-3\right)+\frac{1}{2!}\cdot 3\!\left(x-3\right)^{2}. \end{equation*}
(b) The degree 3 Taylor polynomial, \(T_3 (x)\text{,}\) is obtained by truncating after the \((x-3)^3\) term:
\begin{equation*} T_3 (x) = 5+\left(-3\right)\!\left(x-3\right)+\frac{1}{2!}\cdot 3\!\left(x-3\right)^{2}+\frac{1}{3!}\!\left(-1\right)\!\left(x-3\right)^{3} \end{equation*}
(c) Substitute \(x=3.1\) into the Taylor polynomial of degree 2:
\begin{equation*} T_2(4.9) = 5+\left(-3\right)\!\left(3.1-3\right)+\frac{1}{2!}\cdot 3\!\left(3.1-3\right)^{2} = 4.715. \end{equation*}
From the Taylor polynomial of degree 3, we obtain
\begin{equation*} T_3(4.9) = 5+\left(-3\right)\!\left(3.1-3\right)+\frac{1}{2!}\cdot 3\!\left(3.1-3\right)^{2}+\frac{1}{3!}\!\left(-1\right)\!\left(3.1-3\right)^{3} = 4.71483. \end{equation*}
