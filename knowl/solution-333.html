Solution 5.4.7.3.1.

Let \(u=\ln\!\left(-6x\right)\) and \(dv=x^{8}dx\text{.}\)
Then \(du=\frac{1}{x}dx\) and \(v=\frac{1}{9} x^{9}\text{.}\)
The integration by parts formula says that
\begin{equation*} \int u \, dv = u\cdot v - \int v \, du \end{equation*}
So, substituting in, we get that
\begin{equation*} \int x^8 \ln(-6 x) \, dx = \ln\!\left(-6x\right) \cdot \frac{1}{9}x^{9} - \int \frac{1}{9}x^{9} \cdot \frac{1}{x} \, dx \end{equation*}
\begin{equation*} =\ln\!\left(-6x\right) \cdot \frac{1}{9}x^{9} - \int \frac{1}{9}x^{8} \, dx \end{equation*}
Lastly, we integrate the second part, so the answer is
\begin{equation*} \int x^8 \ln(-6 x) \, dx = \frac{1}{9}x^{9} \cdot \ln\!\left(-6x\right) - \frac{1}{81}x^{9}+C \end{equation*}
