Solution 6.2.5.2.1.

a. We slice the object into disks horizontally. A typical disk has thickness \(\Delta y\) and radius \(x=\root 7\of{y}\text{.}\) Thus
\begin{equation*} \hbox{Volume of slice }\approx \pi x^2\Delta y =\pi y^{2/7}\,\Delta y, \end{equation*}
and
\begin{equation*} \hbox{Volume of solid } = \lim_{\Delta y\to 0}\Sigma\pi y^{2/7}\,\Delta y = \int_0^1 \pi y^{2/7}\, dy \end{equation*}
\begin{equation*} = {7 \pi\over 9} y^{1 + 2/7}\bigg|_0^1 = {7 \pi\over 9}. \end{equation*}
b. We slice the region into vertical rings. A typical ring has thickness \(\Delta x\text{,}\) outer radius \(y=1\) and inner radius \(y=x^{7}\text{.}\) Therefore,
\begin{equation*} \hbox{Volume of slice }\approx \pi 1^2\Delta x-\pi y^2\Delta x = \pi(1-x^{14})\, \Delta x. \end{equation*}
Therefore,
\begin{equation*} \hbox{Volume of solid } = \lim_{\Delta x\to0} \Sigma \pi(1-x^{14})\,\Delta x = \int_0^1\pi(1-x^{14})\, dx \end{equation*}
\begin{equation*} =\pi\left(x-\frac{x^{15}}{15}\right)\Bigg|_0^1 = \pi(1 - {1\over 15}) = {14\over15} \pi. \end{equation*}
c.The region is cylindrical with a hole around the axis of rotation, \(y=-4\text{.}\) We slice it into rings vertically. A typical ring has thickness \(\Delta x\text{,}\) outer radius \(1+4=5\text{,}\) and inner radius \(y+4=x^{7}+4\text{.}\) Thus
\begin{equation*} \hbox{Volume of slice }\approx \pi 5^2\Delta x - \pi(x^{7}+4)^2\Delta x = \pi(9 - x^{14} - 8 x^{7})\, \Delta x. \end{equation*}
Therefore, we have
\begin{equation*} \hbox{Volume of solid } = \int_0^1\pi(9 - x^{14} - 8 x^{7})\,dx \end{equation*}
\begin{equation*} = \pi \left(9 x - \frac{x^{15}}{15} - {1\over 1} x^{8}\right)\Bigg|_0^1 = \pi ( 9 - {1\over 15} - {1\over 1} ). \end{equation*}
