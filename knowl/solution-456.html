Solution 7.6.4.3.1.

(a) We know that a logistic curve can be modeled by the function
\begin{equation*} P=\frac{L}{1+Ce^{-kt}} \end{equation*}
where \(C=(L-P_0)/P_0\) and \(P\) is the number of people infected by the virus at a particular time \(t\text{.}\) We know that \(L\) is the limiting value, or the maximal number of people infected with the virus, so in our case
\begin{equation*} L=7750. \end{equation*}
We are also told that initially there are only 10 people infected with the virus so that we get \(P_0=10.\) Thus we have
\begin{equation*} C = \frac{L-P_0}{P_0} = \frac{7750-10}{10} = 774. \end{equation*}
We are also told that in the early stages of the virus, infection grows exponentially with \(k=1.7\text{.}\) Thus we get that the logistic function for people infected is
\begin{equation*} P=\frac{7750}{1+774 e^{-1.7 t}}. \end{equation*}

Looking at the graph we see that the the point at which the rate changes from increasing to decreasing, the inflection point, occurs at roughly \(t=4\) giving a value of \(P=4000\text{.}\) Thus after roughly 4000 people have been infected, the rate of infection starts dropping.
