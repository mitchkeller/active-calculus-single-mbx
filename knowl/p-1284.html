A. We could use the interval to the left or right of \(x = 2\) to estimate the derivative, or we could average the two. We do the last of these here. For the interval to the left, we have
\begin{equation*} f'(2) \approx \frac{f(2) - f(0)}{2-0} = \frac{-13 + 16}{2} = \frac{3}2. \end{equation*}
For that to the right,
\begin{equation*} f'(2) \approx \frac{f(4) - f(2)}{4-2} = \frac{-12 + 13}{2} = \frac{1}2. \end{equation*}
Our estimate is therefore
\begin{equation*} f'(2) \approx \frac12\left(\frac{3}2 + \frac{1}2\right) = 1. \end{equation*}
