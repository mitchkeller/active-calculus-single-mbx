Since \(g(x)\) is an antiderivative of \(g'(x) = f(x)\text{,}\) by the Second FTC we have
\begin{equation*} g(x) = \int_0^x f(u) \, du\text{.} \end{equation*}
Using our work in (b) and integrating, we now have
\begin{align*} g(x) =\mathstrut \amp \int_0^x f(u) \, du\\ =\mathstrut \amp \int_0^x 1 - u^2 + u^4 - u^6 + \cdots\\ =\mathstrut \amp \left. u - \frac{1}{3}u^3 + \frac{1}{5}u^5 - \frac{1}{7}u^7 + \cdots \right|_0^x\\ =\mathstrut \amp x - \frac{1}{3}x^3 + \frac{1}{5}x^5 - \frac{1}{7}x^7 + \cdots \end{align*}
