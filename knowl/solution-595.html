Solution 9.5.7.1.

\(x = \sin 6 t, \, y = \cos 6 t\text{,}\) hence \(x' = 6 \cos 6 t\) and \(y' = - 6 \sin 6 t\text{.}\) By the formula for the arc length we obtain:
\begin{equation*} S = \int ^\pi _0 \sqrt{x'(t)^2 + y'(t)^2} \, dt = \int ^\pi _0 \sqrt{36 \cos^2 6 t + 36 \sin^2 6 t} \, dt = \int ^\pi _0 \sqrt{36} \, dt = 6 \pi \end{equation*}
