Observe that horizontal slices with thickness \(\Delta y\) are all cylindrical disks, each with radius given by \(r(y) = y^2\) since they are given by the curve \(y = \sqrt{x}\text{,}\) which can be equivalently expressed as \(x = y^2\text{.}\) The volume of each slice is thus
\begin{equation*} V_{\text{slice}} = \pi r(y)^2 \Delta y = \pi y^4 \Delta y\text{.} \end{equation*}
The volume of the solid is thus given by the definite integral
\begin{equation*} V = \int_0^2 \pi y^4 \Delta \, dy\text{.} \end{equation*}
