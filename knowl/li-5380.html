Item a.
When \(k\) is very large, the constant 1 in the numerator is negligible compared to \(k\) and the constant 2 in the denominator is negligible compared to \(k^3\text{.}\) So the numerator looks like \(k\) and the denominator \(k^3\) when \(k\) is large. It follows that \(\frac{k+1}{k^3+2}\) looks like \(\frac{k}{k^3}\) when \(k\) is large.
