We think of the tank as made from infinitely many 'infinitesimal' horizontal slices, with \(x\) the vertical distance an infinitesimal slice of the water is from the bottom of the tank (the vertex of the right circular cone).
A cross section through the vertical center to the side is approximated below (not necessarily to scale).
Now the large right triangle with height 5 and top side length 2 is similar to the right triangle with height \(x\) and top side length \(r\text{.}\) So \(\frac{5}{2} = \frac{x}{r}\implies 5 r = 2 x\) or \(r = {\frac{2}{5}} x\text{.}\)
Therefore the area of every infinitesimal slice is \(A(x) = \pi(r)^2=\pi({\frac{2}{5}} x)^2={\frac{4}{25}} \pi x^2\) \(\text{meter}^2\text{.}\)
The infinitesimal slice has volume \(dV=A(x)dx = {\frac{4}{25}} \pi x^2\,dx\) \(\text{meter}^3\text{,}\) and since mass = density\(\times\) volume, the mass of our infinitesimal slice is \(dm = \delta dV\) where \(\delta= 1050 \frac{kg}{\text{meter}^3}\text{.}\)
So the mass of the slice at \(x\) is \(\delta\,dV= (1050)\left({\frac{4}{25}} \pi x^2\right)\,dx\) kg. We now must find \(mgh\) for each slice.
Each slice at \(x\) must be lifted a height \(h = (5-x)\) meters, and \(g=9.8 \frac{meters}{sec^2}\) . So \(mgh\) for each slice is \(9.8(1050)\left({\frac{4}{25}} \pi x^2\right)(5-x)\,dx\) Joules.
And the sum of lifting all of the slices from the bottom (\(x=0\)) to the top of the water level's depth (\(x=4\)) is
\begin{equation*} \begin{array}{cl} \int_0^{4}(9.8)(1050)\left({\frac{4}{25}} \pi x^2\right)(5-x)\,dx \amp =10290\left({\frac{4}{25}}\right)\pi\int_0^{4} (5 x^2-x^3)\,dx \\ \amp =10290\left({\frac{4}{25}}\right)\pi\left[\frac{5 x^3}{3} - \frac{x^4}{4}\right]_0^{4} \\ \amp =10290\left({\frac{4}{25}}\right)\pi\big[{\frac{320}{3}}-64\big] \\ \amp =10290\left({\frac{4}{25}}\right)\pi\big({\frac{128}{3}}\big) \\ \amp \approx 220686\;\;\text{J} \end{array} \end{equation*}
