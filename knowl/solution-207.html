Solution 3.1.4.2.1.

\(f'(x) > 0\) and \(f'(x) \lt 0\text{;}\) critical points are where \(f'(x) = 0\text{.}\) Here \(f'(x) = 6 + 5 \cos(x)\text{.}\) But \(6 > 5 \cos(x)\) for all \(x\text{,}\) so \(f\) is increasing on \((-\infty,\infty)\) and decreasing nowhere. There are no critical points, because \(f'(x)\) is never zero.
