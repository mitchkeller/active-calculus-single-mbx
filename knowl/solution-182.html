Solution 2.7.3.6.1.

\(x\text{.}\)
\begin{equation*} \frac{1}{\sqrt{1 - x^{10} y^2}} (5 x^{4} y + x^{5} \frac{dy}{dx}) = y^{5} + 5 x y^{4} \frac{dy}{dx}, \end{equation*}
so, clearing the denominator,
\begin{equation*} 5 x^{4} y + x^{5} \frac{dy}{dx} = \sqrt{1 - x^{10} y^2} (y^{5} + 5 x y^{4} \frac{dy}{dx}). \end{equation*}
Thus
\begin{equation*} ( x^{5} - (5 x y^{4}) \sqrt{1 - x^{10} y^2} ) \frac{dy}{dx} = y^{5} \sqrt{1 - x^{10} y^2} - 5 x^{4} y, \end{equation*}
and finally
\begin{equation*} \frac{dy}{dx} = \frac{y^{5} \sqrt{1 - x^{10} y^2} - 5 x^{4} y}{x^{5} - (5 x y^{4}) \sqrt{1 - x^{10} y^2}}. \end{equation*}
