Solution 3.3.4.1.1.

\(g'(x)=0\text{:}\) \(g'(x) = 3 - 2x = 0\text{,}\) so \(x = 1.5\text{.}\) Since \(g'(x)>0\) for \(x\lt 1.5\) and \(g'(x)\lt 0\) for \(x>1.5\text{,}\) the critical point at \(x=1.5\) is a local maximum. As \(x\to\pm \infty\text{,}\) the value of \(g(x)\to-\infty\text{.}\) Thus, the local maximum at \(x=1.5\) is a global maximum of \(g(1.5)=3 \cdot 1.5 - 1.5^2 - 15 = -12.75\text{.}\) There is no global minimum.
