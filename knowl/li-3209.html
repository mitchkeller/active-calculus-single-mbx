Item 4.4.5.15.d.
By considering the cases of \(x \lt 0\) and \(x \gt 0\text{,}\) we see that for \(x \lt 0\text{,}\) \(H'(x) = G'(x) = \frac{1}{x}\) and for \(x \gt 0\text{,}\) \(H'(x) = F'(x) = \frac{1}{x}\) for all \(x \ne 0\text{,}\) the derivative of \(H(x) = \ln(|x|)\) is \(\frac{1}{x}\text{,}\) which makes \(\ln(|x|)\) the antiderivative of \(\frac{1}{x}\text{.}\)
