Item 4.1.5.10.b.
If we think about an interval on which the rate of pollution is relatively constant and suppose the interval is \(\triangle t\) wide, then the area under the curve on that interval is approximately the area of the rectangle whose width is \(\triangle t\) and whose height is \(p(t)\text{,}\) the polution rate on that interval. When we compute the product \(p(t) \cdot \triangle t\) to find the area, the units involved are ``units of pollution per day’’ times ``days’’, which results in an overall unit of ``units of pollution’’. Thus, the area under the curve tells us the total pollution emitted over the time interval \([0,30]\text{.}\)
