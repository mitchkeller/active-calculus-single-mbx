A. The series \(4 + 4 (0.5) + 4 (0.5)^2 + \cdots + 4 (0.5)^{13}\) has 14 terms, and its sum is
\begin{equation*} 4 + 4 (0.5) + 4 (0.5)^2 + \cdots + 4 (0.5)^{13} = {4 (1 - 0.5^{14})\over 1 - 0.5}. \end{equation*}
B. The series \(4 (0.5)^{7} + 4 (0.5)^{8} + 4 (0.5)^{9} + \cdots + 4 (0.5)^{13}\) has \(13 - 7 + 1 = 7\) terms. We can write it as
\begin{equation*} 4 (0.5)^{7} + 4 (0.5)^{8} + 4 (0.5)^{9} + \cdots + 4 (0.5)^{13} = (4 (0.5)^{7}) (1 + 0.5 + \cdots + 0.5^{6}), \end{equation*}
so its sum is
\begin{equation*} (4 (0.5)^{7}) (1 + 0.5 + \cdots + 0.5^{6}) = {4 (0.5)^{7} (1 - 0.5^{7})\over 1 - 0.5}. \end{equation*}
