Solution 3.1.4.4.1.

  1. From the given graph of \(f'\text{,}\) the first derivative sign chart for \(f\) shows that \(f\) has critical numbers at \(x = -1, 1\) and that \(f'\) is positive for \(-1 \lt x \lt 1\) and for \(x \gt 1\text{,}\) while being negative for all \(x \lt -1\text{.}\) Hence \(f\) is increasing for \(-1 \lt x \lt 1\) and for \(x \gt 1\text{,}\) and decreasing for \(x \lt -1\text{.}\) It follows by the first derivative test that \(f\) has a local minimum at \(x = -1\) because \(f\) changes from decreasing to increasing there.

  2. Based on where \(f'(x)\) has horizontal tangent lines and where its slope is positive and negative, we can sketch an approximate graph of \(y = f''(x)\) in the usual way that we sketch the derivative of a given function. From the given graph of \(y = f'(x)\text{,}\) we can see that \(f''(x) = 0\) at \(x \approx -0.35\) and at \(x = 1\text{,}\) and that between these points, \(f''(x)\) is negative because \(f'(x)\) is decreasing, while \(f''(x)\) is positive everywhere else since \(f'(x)\) is increasing everywhere else.   3. From our sketch of the second derivative in (b), we can develop a second derivative sign chart for \(f\text{.}\) In particular, we saw that \(f''(x) = 0\) at \(x \approx -0.35\) and at \(x = 1\text{,}\) and that between these points, \(f''(x)\) is negative, while \(f''(x)\) is positive everywhere else. This shows that \(f\) is concave down for \(-0.35 \lt x \lt 1\) while being concave up everywhere else. We thus see that \(f\) has points of inflection at both \(x \approx -0.35\) and \(x = 1\text{.}\)

  4. Now that we know where \(f\) is increasing and decreasing as well as where \(f\) is concave up and concave down, along with the fact that \(f(0) = -0.5\text{,}\) we can sketch an approximate graph of \(y = f(x)\text{.}\) Note that we also know \(f\) has a horizontal tangent line at \(x = 1\) while being increasing on either side of \(x = 1\text{,}\) and earlier we established that \(f\) has a local minimum at \(x = -1\text{.}\) After making a few rough sketches and shifting the graph vertically to pass through \((0,-0.5)\text{,}\) we arrive at the graph shown at left below.
