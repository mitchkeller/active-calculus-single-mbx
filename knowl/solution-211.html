Solution 3.1.4.8.1.

\(f'(x) = 4\left( 7 x^{6} (7 - x)^{6} - 6 x^{7}(7 - x)^{5} \right)\text{.}\) Critical points are where this is zero, or, where \(4 x^{6} (7-x)^{5} \left( 7(7 - x) - 6 x \right) = 4 x^{6} (7-x)^{5} \left( 49 - 13 x \right) = 0\text{.}\) So critical points are \(x = 0\text{,}\) \(x = 7\) and \(x = 49/13\text{.}\) Because 6, the multiplicity of the root \(x=0\text{,}\) is even, the derivative does not change sign at \(x = 0\text{.}\) Checking points on either side of the other two critical points shows that \(x = 7\) is a local minimum (\(f'\) changes from negative to positive) and \(x = 49/13\) is a local maximum.
