Item 4.1.5.8.b.
Since \(v\) is a positive linear function that connects the points \((0,500)\) and \((15.625,0)\) on the interval \([0, 15.625]\text{,}\) the region enclosed by the velocity function and the \(t\)-axis is a right triangle with vertical leg of length \(500\) and horizontal leg of length \(15.625\text{.}\) The area of this triangle is \(A = \frac{1}{2}bh = \frac{1}{2}(15.625)(500) = 3906.25\text{.}\) Because this is the area under the velocity curve (where velocity is positive) this area represents the vertical distance traveled by the rocket on the time interval \([0, 15.625]\text{.}\)
