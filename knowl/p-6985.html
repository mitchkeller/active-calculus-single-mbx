You can do the next step in different ways, but one way is to solve for \(dx\text{.}\) Since \(du = 10\sec\mathopen{}\left(10x\right)\tan\mathopen{}\left(10x\right) \, dx\text{,}\) we divide over to get that
\begin{equation*} dx=\frac{1}{10\sec\mathopen{}\left(10x\right)\tan\mathopen{}\left(10x\right)} \, du \end{equation*}
Then we substitute back into the original integral.
\begin{equation*} \int \frac{2 \sec(10 x)\tan(10 x)}{\sec^{9}(10 x)} \, dx = \int \frac{2 \sec(10 x)\tan(10 x)}{u^{9}} \frac{1}{10\sec\mathopen{}\left(10x\right)\tan\mathopen{}\left(10x\right)} \, du \end{equation*}
Which simplifies to
\begin{equation*} \int \frac{0.2}{u^{9}}du \end{equation*}
