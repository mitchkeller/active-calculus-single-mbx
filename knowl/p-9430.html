We can see this more formally in Equation (8.3.9) if we divide the two terms in the numerator of \(S_n\) by the denominator. Doing so, an equivalent formula for \(S_n\) is
\begin{equation} S_n = 2 - \frac{1}{2^{n-1}}\text{.}\tag{8.3.10} \end{equation}
In Equation (8.3.10), if we let \(n \to \infty\text{,}\) we have
\begin{equation*} \lim_{n \to \infty} S_n = \lim_{n \to \infty} \left( 2 - \frac{1}{2^{n-1}} \right) = 2\text{,} \end{equation*}
since \(\frac{1}{2^{n-1}} \to 0\) as \(n\) increases without bound.
