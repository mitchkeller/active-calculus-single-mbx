Solution 1.6.5.8.1.

SOLUTION
We can interpret \(f'(x)\) as the slope of the curve \(y = f(x)\) at the point \((x, f(x))\text{.}\) Therefore \(f'(x)\) is negative when \(y = f(x)\) has negative slope and positive when \(y = f(x)\) has positive slope.
Similarly, \(f''(x)\) is negative when \(y= f'(x)\) has negative slope and positive when \(y = f'(x)\) has positive slope.
It follows that B is the graph of the function, A is the graph of the function’s first derivative and C is the graph of the function’s second derivative.
