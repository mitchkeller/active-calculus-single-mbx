(a) Since \(1 - \frac{x^2}{2!} + \frac{x^4}{4!} - \cdots\) is the Taylor series for \(\cos(x)\text{,}\) we know its sum:
\begin{equation*} 1 - \frac{x^2}{2!} + \frac{x^4}{4!} - \cdots = \cos(x) = 0.1. \end{equation*}
Solving for \(x\text{,}\) we get \(x = \arccos(0.1)\text{.}\) (b) Similarly, since \(1 + x + \frac{x^2}{2!} + \frac{x^3}{3!} + \cdots\) is the Taylor series for \(e^x\text{,}\) we know its sum as well:
\begin{equation*} 1 + x + \frac{x^2}{2!} + \frac{x^3}{3!} + \cdots = e^x = 6. \end{equation*}
Solving for \(x\text{,}\) we get \(x = \ln(6)\text{.}\)
