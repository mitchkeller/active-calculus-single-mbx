First, \(P(7)-P(0) = 115(1.014)^7-115(1.014)^0 \approx 0.1175\) billion people is the total population change. It follows that the average rate of change over this time period is \(AV_{[0,7]}=\frac{0.1175}{7} \approx 0.01679\) billion people per year. The instantaneous rate of change of the population at \(t = 7\) is
\begin{equation*} P'(7) = \lim_{h \to 0} AV_{[7,7+h]} = \lim_{h \to 0} \frac{115(1.014)^{7+h}-115(1.014)^7}{h}\text{,} \end{equation*}
and approximating this quantity with small values of \(h\text{,}\) it follows that \(P'(7) \approx 0.1762\text{.}\) Hence, the instantaneous rate of change at \(t = 7\) is greater than the average rate of change of \(P\)on the interval \([0,7]\text{.}\)
