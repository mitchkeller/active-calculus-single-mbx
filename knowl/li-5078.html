Item 8.1.3.5.b.
The graph of \(f\) on the interval \([1, 10]\) and the graph of the sequence \(\left\{\frac{n}{\ln(n)}\right\}\) for \(n\) from 1 to 10 are shown in the figure below. Since the graph of the sequence follows the graph of the function \(f\text{,}\) and since \(f\) is a strictly decreasing function, we should expect that if \(\lim_{x \to \infty} f(x) = L\text{,}\) then \(\lim_{n \to \infty} \frac{\ln(n)}{n} = L\) as well.
