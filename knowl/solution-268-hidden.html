A. The integral is the area above the \(x\)-axis minus the area below the \(x\)-axis. Thus, we can see that \(\int_{-3}^3 f(x)\,dx\) is about \(-6.5 + 1.75 = -4.75\text{.}\) To determine which average value of \(f(x)\) is largest, we consider the areas \(\int_{-3}^{3} f(x) dx\) and \(\int_{0}^{3} f(x) dx\text{.}\) If the first (divided by six) is greater than the second (divided by 3), the average value over the first is larger than that over the second. Here, the interval \(0 \le x \le 3\) gives the largest average value.
