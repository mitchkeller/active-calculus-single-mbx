Item d.
In our current example, \(A\) is an antiderivative of \(f\) that satisfies \(A(0) = -\frac{1}{2} - \frac{\pi}{4}\text{.}\) Our earlier work with \(F\) showed that \(F\) is an antiderivative of \(F\) that satisfied \(F(0) = -1\text{.}\) Since \(F\) and \(A\) are both antiderivatives of \(f\text{,}\) they differ by a constant, and that constant is \(-1 - (-\frac{1}{2} - \frac{\pi}{4}) = \frac{\pi}{4} - \frac{1}{2}\text{.}\)
