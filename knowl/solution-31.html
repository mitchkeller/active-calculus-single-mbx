Solution 1.3.3.2.1.

A. Since \(f\) is increasing, \(f(8)\) < \(f(10)\text{.}\) B. From the figure, it appears that \(f(8) - f(6)\) < \(f(6) - f(4)\text{.}\) C. The quantity \(\frac{f(6)-f(4)}{6-4}\) represents the slope of the secant line connecting the points on the graph at \(x=4\) and \(x=6\text{.}\) This is greater than the slope of the secant line connecting the points at \(x=4\) and \(x=8\text{,}\) which is \(\frac{f(8)-f(4)}{8-4}\) D. The function is steeper at \(x=4\) than at \(x=10\text{,}\) so \(f'(4)\) > \(f'(10)\text{.}\)
