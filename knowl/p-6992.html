Now, this is one of our basic integrals, and we can integrate it. Constants in front just stay multiplied so we get
\begin{equation*} \int 3e^{u}du = 3e^{u}+C \end{equation*}
Finally, make sure you put it back in terms of the original variable, which in this case is ’x’. So we substitute back in for ’u’, which was \(\cot\mathopen{}\left(-2x\right)\text{.}\) Thus the final answer to this integral is
\begin{equation*} \int 6 \csc^2(-2 x) e^{\cot(-2 x)} \, dx = 3e^{\cot\mathopen{}\left(-2x\right)}+C \end{equation*}
