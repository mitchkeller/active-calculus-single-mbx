Item 8.2.4.7.a.
Using the given information that \(f(0) = 2, f'(0) = -3, f''(0) = -1, f'''(0) = 0, f^{(4)}(0) = -3\) and the fact that the coeffficients of the Taylor polynomial centered at \(a = 0\) are \(c_k = \frac{f^{k}(0)}{k!}\text{,}\) we find that
\begin{equation*} T_4(x) = 2 - 3x - \frac{1}{2!}x^2 - \frac{3}{4!}x^4\text{.} \end{equation*}
