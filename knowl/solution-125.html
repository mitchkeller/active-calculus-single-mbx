Solution 2.3.5.1.1.

The function \(y=7x^{3}\mathopen{}\left(x^{2}-8\right)\) is a product of two functions, say \(f(x)= 7x^{3}\) and \(g(x)=x^{2}-8\text{.}\) Then taking the derivatives we get \(f'(x)=7\cdot 3x^{2}\) and \(g'(x)=2x\text{.}\) Finally, the product rule says that
\begin{equation*} \left[f(x)g(x)\right]'=f'(x)g(x)+g'(x)f(x) \end{equation*}
so \(y'= (7\cdot 3x^{2}) \cdot (x^{2}-8) + (2x)\cdot (7x^{3})\text{.}\)
