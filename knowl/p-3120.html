Differentiating implicitly with respect to \(x\) gives

\begin{equation*} 3 y^3 + 9 x y^2 \, \frac{dy}{dx} + y + x \, \frac{dy}{dx} = 0. \end{equation*}

If \(x = 4\) and \(y = 1\text{,}\) then this equation becomes

\begin{equation*} 4 + 40 \, \frac{dy}{dx} = 0, \qquad \text{and so} \qquad \frac{dy}{dx} = -{\frac{1}{10}}. \end{equation*}

If \((x, y)\) is any point on the tangent line besides \(\left(4,1\right)\) then

\begin{equation*} -{\frac{1}{10}} = \frac{y-1}{x-4}, \qquad \text{or} \qquad 1\!\left(x-4\right) = -10\!\left(y-1\right). \end{equation*}

Therefore, \(x+10y = 14\) is an equation of the line tangent to the curve at the point \(\left(4,1\right)\text{.}\)
