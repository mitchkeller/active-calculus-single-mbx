Item d.
\(T_4(x)\) involves the term \(w_4 x^4\text{.}\) When we differentiate this function \(4\) times, we get \(4 \cdot 3 \cdot 2 \cdot 1 w_4\text{.}\) Since \(f^{(4)}(x) = e^x\text{,}\) \(f^{(4)}(0) = 1\text{,}\) so \(4 \cdot 3 \cdot 2 \cdot 1 w_4 = 1\) and therefore \(w_4 = \frac{1}{24}\text{.}\) This means that
\begin{equation*} T_4(x) = 1 + x + \frac{1}{2}x^2 + \frac{1}{6} x^3 + \frac{1}{24}x^4\text{.} \end{equation*}
Plotting \(T_4(x)\) along with the earlier approximations we see that \(T_4(x)\) is an even better approximation to \(f(x) = e^x\) and on a still wider interval.
