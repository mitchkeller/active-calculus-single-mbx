Solution 4.3.5.1.1.

\(y = f(x)\) and the \(x\)-axis, with area below the axis being counted as negative area. Therefore \(\int_a^b f(x) dx = -39\text{,}\) \(\int_b^c f(x) dx = 13\text{,}\) \(\int_a^c f(x) dx = -39 + 13 = -26\text{,}\) and \(\int_a^c |f(x)| dx = |-39| + |13| = 52\text{.}\)
