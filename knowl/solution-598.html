Solution 9.6.5.1.

\(x\) and \(y\) as being parametrically equations with parameter \(\theta\text{:}\)
\begin{equation*} x = r\cos(\theta) = 3 \sin(4\theta) \cos(\theta), \ \hbox{and}\ y = r\sin(\theta) = 3 \sin(4\theta) \sin(\theta). \end{equation*}
Then we know that the slope of the tangent line is \({dy\over dx} = {dy/d\theta\over dx/d\theta}\text{.}\) Taking the derivatives, \({dx\over d\theta} = 3(4\cos(4\theta)\cos(\theta) - \sin(4\theta)\sin(\theta))\) and \({dy\over d\theta} = 3(4\cos(4\theta)\sin(\theta) + \sin(4\theta)\cos(\theta))\text{.}\) Thus
\begin{equation*} {dy\over dx} = {4\cos(4\theta)\sin(\theta) + \sin(4\theta)\cos(\theta) \over 4\cos(4\theta)\cos(\theta) - \sin(4\theta)\sin(\theta) }. \end{equation*}
We want this at \(\theta = \pi/6\text{,}\) so that we get \({dy\over dx}\approx 0.115\text{.}\) Then, at \(\theta = \pi/6\text{,}\) \(x(\pi/6) = 3 \sin(4\cdot \pi/6)\cos(\pi/6) \approx 2.25\text{,}\) and \(y(\pi/6) = 3 \sin(4\cdot \pi/6)\sin(\pi/6) \approx 1.299\text{,}\) so that the tangent line is
\begin{equation*} y \approx 1.299 + (0.115)(x - 2.25). \end{equation*}
(Note, however, that the correct answer may require more significant figures than are shown in this solution.)
