Item 8.4.6.5.a.
Since \(\sum_{k=0}^{\infty} (-1)^{k} \frac{1}{2k+1}\) is an alternating series and \(\frac{1}{2k+1} \to 0\text{,}\) the series converges by the Alternating Series Test. However, the corresponding series of positive terms \(\sum_{k=0}^{\infty} \frac{1}{2k+1}\) diverges by comparison to the Harmonic series.
