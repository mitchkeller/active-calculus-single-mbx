By being twice differentiable, we know that \(g'(x) = 0\) any time \(x\) is a critical number of \(g\text{.}\) Thus, given the signs of \(g'(x)\) on the stated intervals, \(g\) is decreasing before \(x = a\) and decreasing on \(a \lt x \lt b\text{,}\) with a horizontal tangent line at \(x = a\text{.}\) In addition, \(g\) is increasing for \(x \gt b\text{.}\) Thus, we know that \(g\) has a global minimum at \(x = b\text{.}\)
