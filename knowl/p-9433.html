An infinite geometric series \(S\) is a sum of the form
\begin{equation} S = a + ar + ar^2 + \cdots + ar^{n-1} + \cdots\text{,}\tag{8.3.11} \end{equation}
where \(a\) and \(r\) are real numbers. If \(|r| \lt 1\text{,}\) then the infinite geometric series converges to the finite value
\begin{equation} S = \frac{a}{1-r}\text{.}\tag{8.3.12} \end{equation}
If \(|r| \ge 1\text{,}\) then the infinite geometric series does not converge to a finite value.
