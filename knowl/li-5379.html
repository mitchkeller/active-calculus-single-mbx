Item c.

Since \(\frac{a_{k+1}}{a_k} \approx \frac{2}{3}\) for large \(k\text{,}\) the series \(\sum \frac{2^k}{3^k-k}\) is approximately the same as \(\sum \left(\frac{2}{3}\right)^k\) when \(k\) is large. So the series \(\sum \frac{2^k}{3^k-k}\) is approximately geometric with ratio \(\frac{2}{3}\) when \(k\) is large. Since the series \(\sum \left(\frac{2}{3}\right)^k\) converges because the ratio is less than 1, we expect that the series \(\sum \frac{2^k}{3^k-k}\) will also converge.
