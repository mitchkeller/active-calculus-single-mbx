Item 2.2.3.7.d.
Combining our results in (b) and (c), we have found that
\begin{equation*} f'(x) = \sin(x) \cdot \lim_{h \to 0} \left( \frac{\cos(h)-1}{h} \right) + \cos(x) \cdot \lim_{h \to 0} \left( \frac{\sin(h)}{h} \right) = \sin(x) \cdot 0 + \cos(x) \cdot 1 = \cos(x)\text{.} \end{equation*}
