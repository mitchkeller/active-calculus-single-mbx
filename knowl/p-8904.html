First, we construct a picture of the tank as described. Below we see a sketch of \(f(x) = 3\cos \left( \frac{x^3}{4} \right)\) in the setting where the positive \(x\)-axis points down; note that this function first crosses the \(x\)-axis at about \(1.84527\text{.}\) We reflect the curve from the first quadrant across the positive \(x\)-axis to generate a solid of revolution. Cross-sections perpendicular to the \(x\)-axis have thickness \(\triangle x\) and are circular disks.
