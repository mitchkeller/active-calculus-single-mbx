Item 7.4.3.6.c.

If we let \(5730\) years pass from time \(0\text{,}\) the remaining mass of the radioactive sample will be \(\frac{1}{2}M_0\text{.}\) This allows us to find the value of \(k\) by setting \(M(5730)\) equal to \(\frac{1}{2}M_0\) and solving for \(t\text{.}\) Doing so, we have \(\frac{1}{2}M_0 = M_0e^{5730k}\text{,}\) and thus \(\frac{1}{2} = e^{5730k}\text{.}\) It follows that

\begin{equation*} \ln\left(\frac{1}{2}\right) = 5730k \end{equation*}

from which we find \(k = \frac{1}{5730}\ln\left(\frac{1}{2}\right) = -\frac{\ln(2)}{5730}\text{.}\) Hence,

\begin{equation*} M(t) = M_0e^{-\frac{\ln(2)}{5730}t} \approx M_0e^{-0.000121t} \end{equation*}
