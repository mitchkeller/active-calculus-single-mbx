Since
\begin{equation*} \int_0^1 e^{-x^2} \, dx = 1 - \frac{1}{3} + \frac{1}{5 \cdot 2!} - \frac{1}{7 \cdot 3!} + \cdots + \frac{(-1)^n}{(2n+1) n!} + \cdots\text{,} \end{equation*}
we can truncate the alternating series to get a good estimate of the integral. We note that \(\frac{1}{11 \cdot 5!} = \frac{1}{1320} = 0.0007575\ldots\text{,}\) and the next term after this one is less than \(0.0001\text{,}\) so that
\begin{equation*} \int_0^1 e^{-x^2} \, dx \approx 1 - \frac{1}{3} + \frac{1}{5 \cdot 2!} - \frac{1}{7 \cdot 3!} + \frac{1}{9 \cdot 4!} - \frac{1}{11 \cdot 5!} = \frac{31049}{41580} = 0.7467291967291967291\ldots \end{equation*}
A computational device reports that \(\int_0^1 e^{-x^2} \, dx \approx 0.746824\text{.}\)
