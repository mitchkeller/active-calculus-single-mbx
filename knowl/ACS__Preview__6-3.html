Preview Activity 6.3.1.

In each of the following scenarios, we consider the distribution of a quantity along an axis.
Part a
Suppose that the function \(c(x) = 200 + 100 e^{-0.1x}\) models the density of traffic on a straight road, measured in cars per mile, where \(x\) is number of miles east of a major interchange, and consider the definite integral \(\int_0^2 (200 + 100 e^{-0.1x}) \, dx\text{.}\)
(i) What are the units on the product \(c(x) \cdot \triangle x\text{?}\)
Units:
  • cars
  • miles
  • cars/mile
  • miles/car
  • cars*cars
  • miles*miles
(ii) What are the units on the definite integral and its Riemann sum approximation given by
\begin{equation*} \int_0^2 c(x) \, dx \approx \sum_{i=1}^n c(x_i) \triangle x? \end{equation*}
Units:
  • cars
  • miles
  • cars/mile
  • miles/car
  • cars*cars
  • miles*miles
(iii) Evaluate the definite integral \(\int_0^2 c(x) \, dx = \int_0^2 (200 + 100 e^{-0.1x}) \, dx =\)
What does the answer to the definite integral \(\int_0^2 c(x) \, dx\) mean?
  • The number of cars in the first two miles.
  • The number of cars at mile 2.
  • The density of cars in the first two miles.
  • The density of cars at mile 2.
  • The area the cars occupy in the first two miles.
  • The area the cars occupy at mile 2.
Part b
On a 6 foot long shelf filled with books, the function \(B\) models the distribution of the weight of the books, measured in pounds per inch, where \(x\) is the number of inches from the left end of the bookshelf. Let \(B(x)\) be given by the rule \(\displaystyle B(x) = 0.5 + \frac{1}{(x+1)^2}\text{.}\)
(i) What are the units on the product \(B(x) \cdot \triangle x\text{?}\)
Units:
  • pounds
  • inches
  • pound/inch
  • inch/pound
  • pound*pound
  • inch*inch
(ii) What are the units on the definite integral and its Riemann sum approximation given by
\begin{equation*} \int_{12}^{36} B(x) \, dx \approx \sum_{i=1}^n B(x_i) \triangle x? \end{equation*}
Units:
  • pounds
  • inches
  • pound/inch
  • inch/pound
  • pound*pound
  • inch*inch
(iii) Evaluate the definite integral \(\displaystyle \int_{0}^{72} B(x) \, dx = \int_0^{72} (0.5 + \frac{1}{(x+1)^2}) \, dx=\)
What does the answer to the definite integral \(\int_0^{72} B(x) \, dx\) mean?
  • The total weight of the books on the entire shelf.
  • The total weight of the books at the end of the shelf.
  • The distribution of the weight of the books on the entire shelf.
  • The distribution of the weight of the books at the end of the shelf.
  • The area the books occupy on the entire shelf.
  • The area the books occupy at the end of the shelf.
