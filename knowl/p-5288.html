  1. On \((0,1)\text{,}\) \(s\) is increasing because velocity is positive.
  2. \(s(t) = 32t - 16t^2\text{.}\)
  3. \(s(1) - s(\frac{1}{2}) = 4\text{.}\)
  4. \(A = 4\) feet is the total distance the ball traveled vertically on \([\frac{1}{2},1]\text{.}\)
  5. \(s(1) - s(0) = 16\) is the vertical distance the ball traveled on the interval \([0,1]\text{.}\) Equivalently, the area between the velocity curve and the \(t\)-axis on \([0,1]\) is \(A = 16\) feet.
  6. \(s(2) - s(0) = 0\text{,}\) so the ball has zero change in position on the interval \([0,2]\text{.}\)
