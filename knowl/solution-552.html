Solution 9.3.5.1.

The integral involves \(\tan u\) and \(\sec u\) where the exponent of \(\sec u\) is even and positive, so B. Then to integrate, we rewrite in order to have one copy of \(\sec^2(14 x)\) for the derivative of \(u= \tan(14 x)\text{.}\) , and use a trigonometric identity, C, to turn the remaining even powers of \(\sec(14 x)\) into an expression of \(\tan^2(14 x)\text{.}\)
