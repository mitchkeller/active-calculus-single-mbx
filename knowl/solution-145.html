Solution 2.4.3.8.1.

  1. The object’s instantaneous velocity at \(t=2\) is given by \(h'(2)\text{.}\) Since \(h(t) = 3 + \frac{2\cos(t)}{1.2^t}\text{,}\) by the quotient rule we can determine that
    \begin{align*} h'(t) &= \frac{1.2^t \cdot (-2\sin(t)) - 2\cos(t) \cdot 1.2^t \ln(1.2)}{(1.2^t)^2}\\ &= \frac{1.2^t (-2\sin(t) - 2\cos(t) \ln(1.2))}{(1.2^t)^2}\\ &= \frac{-2\sin(t) - 2\cos(t) \ln(1.2)}{1.2^t}\text{.} \end{align*}
    Thus, \(h'(2) = \frac{-2\sin(2) - 2\cos(2) \ln(1.2)}{1.2^2} \approx -1.1575\) feet per second is the instanateous (vertical) velocity of the object at \(t=2\text{.}\)
  2. To find the object’s acceleration at \(t=2\text{,}\) we compute \(h''(2)\text{.}\) First, by the quotient rule,
    \begin{align*} h''(t) &= \frac{1.2^t(-2\cos(t) + 2\sin(t) \ln(1.2)) - (-2\sin(t) - 2\cos(t) \ln(1.2))1.2^t \ln(1.2)}{(1.2^t)^2}\\ &= \frac{-2\cos(t) + 2\sin(t) \ln(1.2)) + 2\sin(t)\ln(1.2) + 2\cos(t) \ln^2(1.2)}{1.2^t}\\ &= \frac{\cos(t)(-2 + 2\ln^2(1.2)) + 4\ln(1.2)\sin(t))}{1.2^t}\text{.} \end{align*}
    Therefore, the acceleration at \(t = 2\) is \(h''(2) = \frac{\cos(2)(-2 + 2\ln^2(1.2)) + 4\ln(1.2)\sin(2))}{1.2^2} \approx 1.0193\) feet per second per second.
  3. At \(t=2\text{,}\) the object is falling and slowing down. We know the object is falling because its velocity is negative, and slowing down because its negative velocity is increasing (due to \(h''(2)\) being positive) and thus getting less negative.
