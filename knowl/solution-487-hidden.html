\(T_2(x)\) is the second degree Taylor polynomial for \(f(x)\) about \(x=0\text{,}\) \(T_2(0)=f(0)\text{,}\) which says \(c_0=f(0)\text{.}\) Since \(\frac{d}{dx}T_2(x) \big|_{x=0} = f'(0)\text{,}\) \(c_1=f'(0)\text{;}\) and since \(\frac{d^2}{dx^2} T_2(x) \big|_{x=0} = f''(0)\text{,}\) \(2c_2=f''(0)\text{.}\) In other words, \(c_0\) is the \(y\)-intercept of \(f(x)\text{,}\) \(c_1\) is the slope of the tangent line to \(f(x)\) at \(x=0\) and \(c_2\) tells us the concavity of \(f(x)\) near \(x=0\text{.}\) Thus \(c_0 \lt 0\text{;}\) \(c_1 > 0\text{;}\) and \(c_2 > 0\)
