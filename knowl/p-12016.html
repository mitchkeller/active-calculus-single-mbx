Then let \(u= \csc(-2 x)\text{,}\) which means that \(du= + 2 \csc(-2 x)\cot(-2 x) \, dx\text{,}\) and thus \(dx= \frac{du}{ + 2\csc(-2 x)\cot(-2 x)}\text{.}\)
\begin{equation*} \begin{aligned} \int 12\cot^{3}\mathopen{}\left(-2x\right)\csc^{3}\mathopen{}\left(-2x\right) \, dx \amp = 12 \int \cot^2(-2 x) \csc^2(-2 x) \csc(-2 x) \cot(-2 x) \, dx \\\\ \amp = 12 \int \bigg( \csc^2(-2 x)-1 \bigg) \csc^2(-2 x) \csc(-2 x) \cot(-2 x) \, dx \\\\ \amp = 12 \int \bigg( u^2-1 \bigg) u^2 \csc(-2 x) \cot(-2 x) \frac{du}{ + 2\csc(-2 x)\cot(-2 x)} \\\\ \amp = \frac{12}{ + 2} \int \bigg( u^2-1 \bigg) u^2 \, du \\\\ \amp = \frac{12}{ + 2} \int \bigg( u^4-u^2 \bigg) \, du \\\\ \amp = \frac{12}{ + 2} \bigg[\frac{ u^5}{5}-\frac{u^3}{3}\bigg]+C \\\\ \amp = \frac{12}{ + 2} \bigg[\frac{ \csc^5(-2 x)}{5}-\frac{\csc^3(-2 x)}{3}\bigg]+C \end{aligned} \end{equation*}
