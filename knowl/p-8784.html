As \(t \to \infty\text{,}\) we know \(e^{-\frac{3}{100}t} \to 0\text{,}\) and thus \(S(t) \to 2000\text{.}\) This makes sense because the inflow carries \(20\) grams of salt per liter continuously. As time goes on, the concentration of solution in the tank should tend to that same concentration, and with \(100\) gallons of solution, the amount of salt will tend to \(20 \cdot 100 = 2000\) grams. This value also matches the stable equilibrium that we found in (f).
