Item 2.5.5.14.a.
We are asked to find \(\frac{dV}{dh}\) (the derivative of \(V\) with respect to the independent variable \(h\)) at the instant \(h = 1\text{.}\) If we first expand \(V\) to write
\begin{equation*} V = \frac{\pi}{3}\left(12h^2 - h^3\right) \end{equation*}
we find that
\begin{equation*} \frac{dV}{dh} = \frac{\pi}{3} \left(24h - 3h^2\right) = \pi \left(8h-h^2 \right)\text{,} \end{equation*}
with units ``cubic feet per foot’’. Evaluating at \(h=1\text{,}\) we get
\begin{equation*} \left. \frac{dV}{dh} \right|_{h=1} = \pi \left(8 \cdot 1 - 1^2 \right) = 7\pi \end{equation*}
