Exercise 10.

In Example 8.2.6, we found that
\begin{equation*} f(x) = \ln(1+x) \approx T_5(x) = 1x - \frac{1}{2}x^2 + \frac{1}{3}x^3 - \frac{1}{4}x^4 + \frac{1}{5}x^5\text{,} \end{equation*}
where \(T_5\) is the degree \(5\) Taylor approximation of \(\ln(1+x)\) centered at \(a = 0\text{.}\)
In Activity 8.2.3, we found that
\begin{equation*} g(x) = \ln(x) \approx P_5(x) = 1(x-1) - \frac{1}{2}(x-1)^2 + \frac{1}{3}(x-1)^3 - \frac{1}{4}(x-1)^4 + \frac{1}{5}(x-1)^5\text{,} \end{equation*}
where \(P_5\) is the degree \(5\) Taylor approximation of \(\ln(x)\) centered at \(a = 1\text{.}\) (Here we are using “\(T_5\)” and “\(P_5\)” to distinguish between these two degree \(5\) polynomial approximations of the two different functions \(f(x) = \ln(1+x)\) and \(g(x) = \ln(x)\text{,}\) centered at two different values.)
  1. Note that \(f(0.5) = \ln(1.5)\text{.}\) Use \(T_5(x)\) appropriately to find an estimate of \(\ln(1.5)\text{.}\)
  2. Observe that \(g(1.5) = \ln(1.5)\text{.}\) Use \(P_5(x)\) to estimate \(\ln(1.5)\text{.}\)
  3. Are the estimates of \(\ln(1.5)\) generated by \(T_5(x)\) and \(P_5(x)\) the same or different? Why do you think this happens?
