The volume of a cylinder is \(V= \pi r^2 h\text{.}\) In this problem, the pool isnâ€™t changing sizes, so the radius is constant. Only the height (and thus volume) of the water is changing over time. So plugging in the value of \(r\) gives \(V=9 \pi h\) and taking the derivative with respect to \(t\) of both sides gives
\begin{equation*} \frac{dV}{dt}= 9 \pi \frac{dh}{dt} \end{equation*}
We are asked to find the rate at which the height of the water is changing when the volume is changing at the rate of \(3\) cubic feet per min, so
\begin{equation*} 3=9 \pi \frac{dh}{dt} \end{equation*}
and thus
\begin{equation*} \frac{dh}{dt} = \frac{3}{9 \pi }\ \ \rm{ft/min} \end{equation*}
