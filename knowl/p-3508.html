To find the critical numbers of \(p\text{,}\) we first compute \(p'(x)\text{.}\) Treating \(a\) as constant, we have \(p'(x) = 3x^2 - 2ax = x(3x - 2a)\text{.}\) Thus, setting \(p'(x) = 0\text{,}\) we find that \(x(3x-2a) = 0\) and thus \(x = 0\) and \(x = \frac{2a}{3}\) are the critical numbers of \(p\text{.}\)
