Item b.
With
\begin{equation*} S_n = 1 + \frac{2}{5} + \left(\frac{2}{5}\right)^2 + \cdots + \left(\frac{2}{5}\right)^{n-1} \end{equation*}
and
\begin{equation*} \frac{2}{5} \cdot S_n = \frac{2}{5} + \left(\frac{2}{5}\right)^2 + \left(\frac{2}{5}\right)^3 + \cdots + \left(\frac{2}{5}\right)^{n}\text{,} \end{equation*}
it follows that if we subtract the second equation from the first,
\begin{equation} S_n - \frac{2}{5} \cdot S_n = 1 - \left(\frac{2}{5}\right)^{n}\text{,}\tag{8.3.6} \end{equation}
since every term in \(S_n\) except \(1\) appears in \(\frac{2}{5} \cdot S_n\text{,}\) and every term in \(\frac{2}{5} \cdot S_n\) except \(\left(\frac{2}{5}\right)^{n}\) appears in \(\frac{2}{5} \cdot S_n\text{.}\)
