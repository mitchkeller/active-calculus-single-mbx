Solution 6.1.5.4.1.

Over the interval \([0,\pi]\text{,}\) the graphs \(y = 3\sin x\) and \(y = 9 \cos x\) intersect when \(3\sin x = 9 \cos x\text{,}\) i.e., \(\tan x = \frac{9}{3}\text{,}\) i.e., \(x=\tan^{-1}(\frac{9}{3})=1.24904577239825\text{.}\) Let \(t = \tan^{-1}(\frac{9}{3})\text{.}\) Over the interval \(\left[0, t\right]\text{,}\) the graph of \(y = 3\sin x\) lies below that of \(y = 9 \cos x\text{.}\) Hence, the area between the two curves is \(\int_0^{ t}(9 \cos x-3 \sin x)dx\)
\begin{equation*} \begin{array}{ll} \amp =\left.(9\sin x+3\cos x)\right|_0^{ t} \cr \amp =\left(9\sin t+3\cos t\right)-(9\cdot 0+3\cdot 1)\cr \amp =\left(9\sin t+3\cos t\right)-3 \end{array} \end{equation*}
Since \(t = \tan^{-1}(\frac{9}{3})\text{,}\) we have \(\tan t = \frac{9}{3}\text{.}\) Considering a rightangle triangle with an angle which opposite side has length 9 and adjacent side has length 3, we see that the hypotenues is \(\sqrt{90}\text{.}\) Thus \(\sin t = \frac{9}{\sqrt{90}}\) and \(\cos t = \frac{3}{\sqrt{90}}\text{.}\) \(9\sin t+3\cos t = \frac{9^2}{\sqrt{90}} + \frac{3^2}{\sqrt{90}} = \frac{9^2+3^2}{\sqrt{90}} = \frac{90}{\sqrt{90}}=\sqrt{90}\text{.}\) Thus, \(\int_0^{ t}(9 \cos x-3 \sin x)dx= \sqrt{90}-3.\) Over the interval \(\left[ t,\pi\right]\text{,}\) the graph of \(y = 3\sin x\) lies above that of \(y = 9 \cos x\text{.}\) Hence, the area between the two curves is \(\int^\pi_{ t}(3\sin x - 9 \cos x)dx\)
\begin{equation*} \begin{array}{ll} \amp =-\left.(9\sin x+3\cos x)\right|^\pi_{ t} \cr \amp =3 + \sqrt{90} \end{array} \end{equation*}
Thus the total area is the sum of the above two integrals, which is \(2\sqrt{90}\text{.}\)
