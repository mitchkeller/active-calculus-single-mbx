Item d.
The curves \(y = 2x^2 + 1\) and \(y = x^2 + 4\) intersect at the points \((\pm \sqrt{3}, 7)\text{;}\) after we revolve the region \(S\) about the \(x\)-axis, cross-sections perpendicular to the axis of rotation are washers with outer radius \(R(x) = x^2 + 4\) and inner radius \(r(x) = 2x^2 + 1\text{.}\) By the washer method, we see that
\begin{equation*} V = \int_{-\sqrt{3}}^{\sqrt{3}} \pi( (x^2 + 4)^2 - (2x^2 + 1)^2) \, dx = \frac{136\sqrt{3}}{5}\pi\text{.} \end{equation*}
