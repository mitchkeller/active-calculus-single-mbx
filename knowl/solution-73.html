Solution 1.6.5.5.1.

A. \(f(x) > 0\) at x1,x2,x3, because the graph is above the \(x\)-axis there. B. \(f'(x) > 0\) at x1,x2,x5, because the graph is sloping up there. C. \(f(x)\) is increasing at x1,x2,x5, because the graph is sloping up there. This is the same condition as part (B). D. \(f'(x)\) is increasing at x1,x4,x5, because the graph is bending upward there. E. The slope of \(f(x)\) is negative at x3,x4, because the graph is sloping down there. F. The slope of \(f'(x)\) is negative at x2,x3, because the graph is bending downward there.
