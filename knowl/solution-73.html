Solution 1.7.5.1.1.

(a) As \(x\) approaches \(-1\) from either side, the values of \(f(x)\) get closer and closer to \(-9\text{,}\) so the limit appears to be about \(-9\text{.}\) (b) As \(x\) approaches 0 from either side, the values of \(f(x)\) get closer and closer to \(-10\text{.}\) (Recall that to find a limit, we are interested in what happens to the function near \(x\) but not at \(x\text{.}\)) The limit appears to be about \(-10\text{.}\) (c) As \(x\) approaches 1 from either side, the values of \(f(x)\) get closer and closer to \(-9\) on one side of \(x = 1\) and get closer and closer to \(5\) on the other side of \(x = 1\text{.}\) Thus the limit does not exist. (d) As \(x\) approaches 4 from either side, the values of \(f(x)\) get closer and closer to 17. (Again, recall that we don't care what happens right at \(x = 4\text{.}\)) The limit appears to be about 17.
