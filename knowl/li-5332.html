Item f.
Plotting the function \(T_2(x) = 1 + x + \frac{1}{2} x^2\) along with \(f(x)=e^x\) and \(T_1(x)=1+x\) in Figure 8.1.5, we notice that \(T_2(x)\) is a better approximation to \(f(x) = e^x\) near \(a = 0\) than the tangent line: the quadratic approximation is closer to \(e^x\) for any value of \(x\text{,}\) and the approximation holds for a wider interval of values. For instance, in Preview Activity 8.1.1, we found that \(|f(x)-T_1(x)| \lt 0.1\) for approximately \(-0.5 \lt x \lt 0.4\text{.}\) The quadratica approximation is much better: we note that \(|f(x)-T_2(x)| \lt 0.1\) for approximately \(-0.9 \lt x \lt 0.8\text{,}\) and for any \(x\)-value in that interval, \(|f(x)-T_2(x)| \lt |f(x)-T_1(x)|\text{.}\)
