Let \(q(t) = \displaystyle{\frac{t^{3}+4t}{2t^{2}}}\) and observe that \(q(t) = \displaystyle{\frac{g(t)}{f(t)}}\text{.}\) Rewrite the formula for \(q\) by dividing each term in the numerator by the denominator and simplify to write \(q(t)\) as a sum of constant multiples of powers of \(t\text{.}\) Once you do this, you will have the sum of 2 terms, and you can enter them in either order below.
