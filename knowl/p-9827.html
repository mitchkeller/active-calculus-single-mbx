First, recall that the \(n\)th partial sum is the sum of the first \(n\) terms, so
\begin{equation*} S_n = \sum_{k=0}^{n-1} (-1)^k \left( \frac{4}{5} \right)^k = 1 - \frac{4}{5} + \frac{16}{25} - \cdots + (-1)^{n-1} \left( \frac{4}{5} \right)^{n-1}\text{.} \end{equation*}
We compute the first \(10\) partial sums of the series and display the results in Table 8.5.5 and plot the points \((n, S_n)\) in Figure 8.5.6.
