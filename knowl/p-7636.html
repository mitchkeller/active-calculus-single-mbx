Multiplying by the least common denominator gives
\begin{equation*} -\left(5x^{2}+x+40\right) = A(x^2+9)+(Bx+C)(x - 5) \end{equation*}
Rearranging terms on the right hand side, yields
\begin{equation*} -\left(5x^{2}+x+40\right) = (A+B)x^2+(-5B+C)x + 9 A - 5C \end{equation*}
Now we equate the coefficients:
\begin{equation*} \begin{array}{rl} A+B \amp = -5 \\ -5B +C \amp = -1 \\ 9 A - 5C \amp = -40 \end{array} \end{equation*}
Solving the system gives \(A=-5, \; B = 0\) and \(C= -1\) so the partial fraction decomposition is
\begin{equation*} \frac{-\left(5x^{2}+x+40\right)}{(x - 5)(x^2+9)} = \frac{-5}{x - 5} + \frac{-1}{x^2+9} \end{equation*}
The definite integral is then
\begin{equation*} \displaystyle \int \frac{-\left(5x^{2}+x+40\right)}{(x - 5)(x^2+9)} dx = -5\ln\mathopen{}\left(\left|x-5\right|\right) + \frac{-1\tan^{-1}\mathopen{}\left(\frac{x}{3}\right)}{3} +C \end{equation*}
