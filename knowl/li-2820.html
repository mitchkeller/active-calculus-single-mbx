Item c.
First, using work from and similar to that in (c), we find \(\int_0^5 f(x) \, dx = -3 + 2 = -1\) and \(\int_0^5 g(x) \, dx = 3\text{,}\) thus by the sum rule,
\begin{equation*} \int_0^5 (f(x) + g(x))\, dx = \int_0^5 f(x)\, dx + \int_0^5 g(x)\, dx = -1 + 3 = 2\text{.} \end{equation*}
