Solution 1.8.4.5.1.

\(x=1\text{,}\) we have \(a=1\text{.}\) Since the point with \(x=1\) lies on both the line and the curve, we have
\begin{equation*} f(a) = f(1) = 4(1) - 5 = -1. \end{equation*}
The approximation is an underestimate because the line lies under the curve. Since the linear function approximates \(f(x)\text{,}\) we have
\begin{equation*} f(1.3) \approx 4(1.3) - 5 = 0.2. \end{equation*}
