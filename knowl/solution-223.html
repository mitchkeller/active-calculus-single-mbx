Solution 3.2.3.3.1.

\(p'(x)=2ax - 3x^2 = x(2a - 3x)\text{,}\) so critical points are at \(x = 0\) and \(x = 2a/3\text{.}\) Then \(p''(x) = 2a - 6x\text{,}\) so that plugging in \(x = 0\) or \(x = 2a/3\) we find that \(p''\) is positive or negative, respectively, indicating that \(p\) is concave concave up or down, so that the critical points give a minimum or maximum, respectively. Then, increasing the value of \(a\) moves the critical points away from (for \(x=2a/3\) only) the \(y\)-axis, and the values of \(p\) away from (for \(x=2a/3\) only) the \(x\)-axis.
