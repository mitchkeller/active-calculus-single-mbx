Using \(\cos(x) = 1 - \frac{1}{2!}x^2 + \frac{1}{4!}x^4 - \frac{1}{6!}x^6 + \cdots\text{,}\)
\begin{align*} C(x) =\mathstrut \amp \int_0^x \cos(t^2) \, dt\\ =\mathstrut \amp \int_0^x \left( 1 - \frac{1}{2!}t^4 + \frac{1}{4!}t^8 - \frac{1}{6!}t^{12} + \cdots \right) \, dt\\ =\mathstrut \amp \left. \left( t - \frac{1}{5 \cdot 2!}t^5 + \frac{1}{9 \cdot 4!}t^9 - \frac{1}{13 \cdot 6!}t^{13} + \cdots \right) \right|_0^x\\ =\mathstrut \amp x - \frac{1}{5 \cdot 2!}x^5 + \frac{1}{9 \cdot 4!}x^9 - \frac{1}{13 \cdot 6!}t^{13} + \cdots \end{align*}
